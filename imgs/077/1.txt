Facts:
All developing regions in the world have achieved, or are close to achieving, gender equality in primary education.
Roughly 60% of people will avoid situations where they could be faced with their fear.
Social phobias are often cultural, but they affect people of all races and social classes.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common fears people have in their life, personally and professionally? 
How do people respond when they are afraid? 
What are some things you are hopeful for? 
(EP) 
Is there anything that you used to fear that you don't anymore? How did that happen? 
How have your hopes changed over time? Can you recall a particular time when you got what you hoped for? Tell me about it. 
Have your hopes or fears for the day ever influenced the outcome? How so?
(EX) 
Many people hope to be successful in their careers. Do you think people who put their careers first miss out on other life events? If so, why do you think an individual forgets to prioritize other life events? 
Do you think a person's fears can drive them to do great things? How does a person overcome their fears? Should a person ever give up hope? 
Can a person be too hopeful? Does this make them naïve, in a sense? Why or why not?
