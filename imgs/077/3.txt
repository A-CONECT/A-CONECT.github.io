Facts:
75% of people suffer from speech anxiety prior to giving a presentation or public speech. 
One of the most-viewed TED talks is Dr. Brene Brown's, "The Power of Vulnerability", which suggests people need to embrace their vulnerabilities, rather than avoid them.
C. S. Lewis once said, "To love at all is to be vulnerable".
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
How can you tell someone is being vulnerable? 
Do you think there are ways to avoid being vulnerable? 
What might be some positives to being vulnerable with someone? What are some negatives to being vulnerable with someone?
(EP) 
Have you attended an event or listened to a speech where someone was vulnerable? What was your impression of the event? 
Do you remember your first heartbreak? How did you get through it? 
Have you experienced a serious injury or illness where you had to depend on someone else? What was that like?
(EX) 
Should there be different vulnerability expectations for men and women? 
If someone in a leadership position shows vulnerability, does that make them weak? Is it circumstantial? 
Do you think being vulnerable requires courage? How so?
