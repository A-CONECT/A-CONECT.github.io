Topic: Sunday Comics
Picture Caption:
A young girl reading the newspaper with her family.
Facts:
Charlie Brown started out in a comic called "Li'l Folks." In 1950 the comic was renamed "Peanuts."
The first full-color page of comics appeared in the newspaper "New York World" in 1894.
Popeye, the comic character, was known for getting his strength from spinach. It is believed the popularity of Popeye increased the U.S.'s consumption of spinach by one-third. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Did you or do you read comic strips in the paper? 
Do you have a favorite comic strip? 
What else do you enjoy about the Sunday paper (ex: puzzles, articles, etc.)? 
(EP) 
Did you and your parents ever read comic strips together? Or did you do that with your children? 
What are some of the changes you've noticed in comic strips over the years? 
Was reading the paper an important part of your (or your parents') morning routine? Has this changed? 
(EX) 
In your opinion are family rituals, such as reading the comic strips together, an important part of being a family? Why/why not? 
Do you think the majority of comic strips are geared towards today's population? Or do they seem out of date? Do you think comic strips have kept up with modern society today?
Some comic strips have political subject matter, do you think that the humor present in these comics works as a positive or as a negative in getting the message across? What might improve their message?
