Facts:
A Forbes magazine article suggests eye contact should range from 30% to 60% during a conversation. Too much eye contact can be considered rude, while too little can make a person appear insincere.
Contrary to common belief, a 2007 study found there was little difference in the number of words men and women use in a conversation.
A 2011 Dutch study found four seconds of silence in a conversation is all it takes to make a person feel uncomfortable.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common phrases you use? 
What are some important skills to have for making conversation? 
What makes an effective listener? 
(EP) 
Have you ever had a particularly awkward encounter with a person? What happened? 
Did you know or meet anyone that was very welcoming and instantly easy to talk to? How did you meet them? 
Have you ever realized that your first impression about someone was wrong? How did they change your opinion of them?
(EX) 
News anchors have to effectively communicate to many people every day. What are some pros and cons of this kind of job?
Some people prefer to keep to themselves. Do you think it is important to socialize even if it is uncomfortable? Why is that? 
There are cultural differences in appropriate ways to interact. How do you think interactions in Western Culture differ from other cultures? What are some good and bad habits we have when we interact?
