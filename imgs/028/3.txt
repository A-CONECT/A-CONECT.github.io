Malala Yousafzai giving a speech at a refugee camp. 
Facts:
Malala Yousafzai, the famous woman's rights advocate, is the youngest recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize.
Public speaking remains the number one fear in America, ahead of the fear of spiders and heights.
Public speaking has known benefits including strengthening self-confidence as well as increasing communication and leadership skills. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you know of any good public speakers? 
What skills does a good public speaker have? 
Are you comfortable with public speaking? Why/why not? 
(EP) 
Have you spoken publicly before? What was the occasion? Were you nervous? How much did you practice? 
Did you ever learn an ice breaker that you enjoyed? What ice breaker was it and where did you learn it? 
Have you ever gone to a comedy show? Who did you see and how did the night go? 
(EX) 
Should public speaking be a basic skill that everyone has? Why/ why not? Do you think public speaking should be a required class in school? At what grade level? Why at that grade? If not, why? 
What level of nerves is a healthy amount before a public speech? Why would someone say a different amount? 
At what point do nerves become too overwhelming? How might someone work through that situation?
