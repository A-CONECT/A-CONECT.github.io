Topic: Written Communication Picture Caption:
President Obama writing a letter in the Oval Office.
Facts:
A letter mailed out in 1931 was finally delivered after 83 years. No one is sure how the letter was lost or how it found its way into the mail system again.
According to Obama, while he was president he would receive roughly 40,000 letters every day. 
In 2016, the US Postal Service fleet used more than 650,000 tires. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some different types of written communication? 
What type of written communication do you most commonly use? 
How has delivery of written communication changed over time?
(EP) 
What was the last letter you wrote? Who were you mailing it to? Have you ever had a pen pal? How often would you send letters? 
Have you ever sent or a received post cards from a vacation? Where was it from? Who did you send it to/ sent it to you? 
Did you use to write notes in school? Did you ever get in trouble for passing notes in class?
(EX) 
Do you think it is important to send and receive cards for events like birthdays? Why? Do you think it is important to include a personal message in these cards? Do you think this formality has been lost with because of technology (ex: text messages, emails, etc.)? Why/ why not? 
Many people complain that mail delivery is slow. Do you think this is true, or is today's society too used to getting things quickly? Why do you think that? 
Paperless options for billings and cards are becoming more common. What are the pros and cons of moving towards paperless systems?
