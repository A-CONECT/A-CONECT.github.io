Children/young adults participating in a cooking competition.
Facts:
The largest free kitchen is in India and feeds 100,000 people every day. The food never runs out, and no one is ever turned away.
The potato chip is believed to have originated in New York in 1853 after a customer sent back their potatoes multiple times saying the potato slices were too thick.
For 2018, the majority of professional chefs said they want to increase the number of kid dishes they have on their menus.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What's your favorite meal to cook? What about this meal makes it your favorite? 
What are some of your favorite aspects when it comes to cooking for other people? 
Did you ever consider becoming a professional chef/cook? 
Have or do you know anyone who was a professional chef/cook?
(EP) 
Growing up did you ever take a cooking or home economics class? What was one of fun about it? What was difficult about it? 
What would you say is the fanciest dining experience you've ever had? What was it like? What did you have? 
Was there ever a time when you had to cook, or help cook for a large amount of people? What was the occasion? What did you cook? 
(EX) 
Do you think working as a chef can take some of the fun out of cooking? Is it important to separate hobbies from work? Why/ why not? 
In the past, women primarily did the cooking. Do you think gender roles with cooking are different today? Why/ why not? 
Many people these days have alternative diets, like vegetarian or vegan. Why do you think these alternative diets have gained popularity within recent years?
