A mother teaching her daughter how to cook.
Facts:
Kids who cook are 50% more likely as adults to cook 5 meals a week.
Chef Boyardee, known for his canned pasta, started producing pasta with his brothers. Today, Boyardee's grand-niece is also a chef and has authored a collection of their family recipes.
When asked if people share recipes almost 20% said they would change a recipe or not share a recipe if it was a family secret.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you know how to cook? 
How old were you when you learned how to cook? 
Who taught you how to cook? 
In your family, who were the main cooks? 
What were some of the main meals your family cooked?
(EP) 
What was the first meal you learned how to cook? Did you enjoy it? Why? 
What's the most fancy and simplest meals you've ever cooked? 
Have you ever set a fire while cooking? What happened? 
Have you ever created your own recipe? Do you remember the first time you made this dish? 
What was the last meal you cooked?
(EX) 
At what age do you think children should start cooking? Why? 
A major part of cooking is preparing the ingredients. Do you think children should handle knives? 
Are there gender biases when it comes to cooking? What makes you think this? 
In your opinion, does learning how to cook help teach skills that are able to be used in other areas of life?
