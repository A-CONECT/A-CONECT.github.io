Facts:
The stars on the original American flag were in a circle so all the Colonies would appear equal. 
To avoid cracking it, the Liberty Bell has not been rung since 1846. Every fourth of July it is symbolically tapped 13 times.
The original draft of the Declaration of Independence is lost.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you celebrate Independence Day? 
What are some patriotic symbols you can think of? 
Is Independence Day celebrated differently on the East Coast and West Coast? How so? 
(EP) 
Did you sing patriotic songs in school? What songs were they and how often did you sing them? 
What was Independence Day like where you grew up? Did you attend any local events? 
Have you ever visited an American history museum or monument? What was it like?
(EX) 
What are some reasons people celebrate Independence Day today? Do you think it has lost any of its original meaning? 
Do you think children should be required to learn the pledge of allegiance in school? Why/why not? 
What does it mean to "respect the flag?" Do you think this is the same for everyone?
