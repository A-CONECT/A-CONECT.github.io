A group of children doing arts and crafts.
Facts:
A notable child art prodigy, Wang Yani, had her painting showcased at the Smithsonian at the age of 3.
Students enrolled in high school arts programs are more likely to graduate and go to college.
Play-Doh was initially sold as a wallpaper cleaner to remove soot and dirt.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Did you enjoy making art projects as a child? 
Did you have a preferred/favorite art medium? 
Were you or are any of your children into making art? 
Are there any artists in your family? 
Did any of your or your children's art get hung up on the refrigerator? 
(EP) 
Do you have a favorite art project that you made? What was it? 
What were your art classes like in school growing up? 
Did you ever attempt an art project when you were younger that didn't turn out as you expected? 
Did you ever work on an art project or projects with your children? What were they?
(EX) 
Do you think art is an important subject in elementary or high school? Why/why not? 
Is art something you think should be graded when it comes to children or is the creative process the most important thing? 
Children are often placed into academic courses based on their ability, should art courses be organized the same way? Why/why not?
