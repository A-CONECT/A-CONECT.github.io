Facts:
The "Dr. Seuss House" in Alaska has 12 stories because the designer wanted a view of Denali/Mount McKinley and contemplated adding more stories when the forest trees grew to impede the view.
72% of people create Do-It-Yourself home décor instead of buying items.
The Imperial Palace in China was designed according to principles of Feng Shui with décor and structures in numbers of 5 and 9; for example, there are around 9,999 rooms which symbolize longevity.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you have any art pieces in your home? 
Are there any pieces of art or furniture that you've carried with you throughout your life? 
Is home decorating something that you enjoy? 
Do you prefer a minimalist approach to decorating or a more elaborate one?
(EP) 
Are there any pieces of art that you can remember from your parents' home that stand out to you? 
Do you have a favorite piece of art or furniture? Where and when did you get it? 
Have you ever been to a home where the way it was decorated amazed you? 
What about a home that had the opposite effect on you?
(EX) 
Do you think home décor is essential to making a house or apartment feel like home? Why/why not? 
What are other ways to make a house feel like home? 
Do you think some modern homes are focused too much on design rather than functionality and comfortability (e.g., glass, metal, sharp edges, very clean)? 
Homes can sometimes be seen as a class status symbol. In your opinion, do we place too much importance on material items? Why/why not?
