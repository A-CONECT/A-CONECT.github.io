Facts:
Over the last three decades, the percent of 16-year-olds with drivers’ licenses has dropped by nearly 50%.
Only 18% of Americans know how to drive a stick-shift, or manual transmissions, car.
The part of the brain related to paying attention and making decisions does not fully mature until ones mid 20's. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What was your first car? 
What did you want your first car to be? 
Who taught you how to drive? 
How did you "save up" for your first car? 
How old were you when you first learned to drive? 
What were common first cars in your community or family?
(EP) 
Do you have any memorable experiences learning how to drive? (ex:. driving in a field/empty parking lot, learning how to use a clutch, etc.) 
What was your Driver's test like? Were you nervous? Do you remember if you made any mistakes? 
Have you ever been pulled over? What was that experience like? Were you scared? 
Do you remember any fun trips/adventures you took in your first car?
(EX) 
Are there benefits to learning how to drive in a busy city compared to a less crowded small town? What are the disadvantages? 
Some people rely on public transit as opposed to learning how to drive. Do you think all people should learn how to drive? Why/ why not? 
With technology moving towards self-driving cars do you think it will still be important for people to learn how to drive? Why might someone think differently?
