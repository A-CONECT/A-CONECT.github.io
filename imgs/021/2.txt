Facts:
The safest spot in the car is the middle seat in the back.
Seatbelts were standard equipment as of the late 1960s, but they weren't required for use in the United States until 1984.
The Oldsmobile Tornado became the first car with a passenger airbag in 1973.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What changes have you noticed in the safety features of cars you've owned? 
Did you use your seatbelt much when you first started driving? 
What changes have you noticed in the emphasis on driving safety (ex: seatbelt laws, driving and drinking, driving and smoking, driving and texting, etc.)?
(EP) 
Do you have any friends or family that you consider an "unsafe driver"? Can you recall an instance that validated this? 
Have you ever been involved or witnessed a car crash/ fender bender? 
Have you ever witnessed "road rage" or instances of very unsafe driving? What happened?
(EX) 
Smart cars are very small compact cars. Do you think cars like these on the road are more or less safe than full-sized vehicles? What are some pros and cons to smart cars? 
Self- driving cars are in the process of being available to the public. Would you feel more safe on the road with a human behind the wheel, or a car that drives itself? Why/ why not? 
Do you think it should be mandatory for people to take driving safety classes, such as Drivers Ed, or should such classes remain optional? Why might someone have an opposing view?
