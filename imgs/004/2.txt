Facts:
As of 2017, there are roughly 21 million US veterans. 
The G.I. Bill allowed veterans to get easy home loans. This encouraged millions of American families to move out of apartments and into suburban homes.
In 1990, almost 50% of young Americans had a veteran for a parent. In 2014, only about 15% did.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Have you or anyone you have known used any of the programs offered by the military, such as the G.I. Bill? 
What character traits are built through military service? 
How have views on military service changed over your life time? 
(EP) 
Have you ever been to a veterans memorial service? What do you remember about it? How did they honor him or her? 
Did you know any World War I veterans? Do you recall any memorable war stories? 
Did you or someone you know receive any medals of honor?
(EX) 
Do you think there is an obligation to serve in the military? Why or why not? 
During the 1940s the U.S. began the draft for military service and young people were selected for mandatory service. What were some benefits and/or problems of the draft? 
In some countries, it is mandatory for young adults to serve in the military. Do you think this system is beneficial? Why or why not?
