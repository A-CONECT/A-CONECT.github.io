Facts:
The Army is the second-largest employer in the United States, second only to Wal-Mart.  
Over one-third of all active-duty women are African American. 
The Marine Corps is the only branch of the U.S. Armed Forces that still has separate boot camps for men and women.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What wars have been fought in your lifetime? Is there a war that stands out most to you? 
Have you ever served in the military? If so, what was your role? 
Did you have any friends, family, or ancestors who fought in any wars or who served in the military? 
What military training do you think would be most important?
(EP) 
When you became old enough to serve, what was going on in the world? What recruitment tactics were used that you remember? 
How did you decide to serve or not? 
What moment most influenced your decision to join or not join the military? 
(EX) 
In 1948, the U.S. Military officially made military service open to women. What are some benefits to women serving? What concerns made women's service controversial? 
Should anyone who wants to join the military be allowed to serve? What are some benefits and/or problems with this idea?
Today, men are required by law to register in the military around age 18. Do you think this should still exist? Why or why not?
