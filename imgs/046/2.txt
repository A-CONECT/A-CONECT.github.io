Family of three eating at a family-friendly restaurant in Williamsburg, VA.
Facts:
At the buffet-style restaurant "Sweet Tomatoes", a family of four can eat for about 25 dollars.
Since Red Robin introduced more fruit and vegetable options into their kids' menu, 40% of kids ordered something other than fries.
An online survey found people were split down the middle as to whether there should be child-free spaces in restaurants.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Have you ever known anyone who worked in a restaurant? What did they do? 
What restaurants would you consider appropriate for families? 
How do you figure out how much to tip? What do you consider? 
(EP) 
Was there a memorable time when you went out to eat with your family? 
Have you ever received terrible service at a restaurant? What happened? 
Was there a popular diner near you growing up? What do you remember about it?
(EX) 
Do you think it's important for everyone to try a job in the food industry (e.g., waitressing)? What can someone learn from this experience? Any downsides? 
Should the wage of wait staff be raised to replace tipping? What are some pros and cons to this? 
Is it better to have a variety of food choices near you or simple options? Why might someone think differently? 
Should family restaurants get all of their ingredients from their local farms? In what ways might that be a challenge?
