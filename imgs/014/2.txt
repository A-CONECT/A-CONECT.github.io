Facts:
The number of books in a family's home has a greater impact on a child’s educational success than the parents’ education or economic situation.
Dr. Seuss said he expected to spend "a week or so" writing The Cat in the Hat. It ended up taking a year and a half.
The Tale of Peter Rabbit was rejected by publishers, so the author self-published. Since then, it has sold more than 45 million copies. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What classic children's books are you familiar with? 
What are common themes found in children's books? 
Do you have any favorite characters from children's books?
(EP) 
Do you have any memories of reading stories to children or with your family? What were you reading? 
Do you remember any stories you were told as a child? Do you remember any stories you told children? 
Have you ever written a story? What was it about?
(EX) 
Do you think there are books or stories that everyone should read in school? Why or why not? What are they? 
What are some benefits and challenges to reading books to children? 
Do you think the moral lessons taught in children's books are too simplified? Why might others think differently?
