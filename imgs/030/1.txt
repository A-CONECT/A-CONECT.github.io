Facts:
The smile may be the most deceptive facial expression. There are 18 different smile types, but only one reflects true happiness.
There are 6 universal facial expressions: Happy, sad, angry, disgusted, surprised, and afraid.
Eye contact produces a powerful sense of connection that even extends to drawn or photographed eyes.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some common facial expressions? What do they mean? 
Do you have a good "poker face"?
 What facial expression do you think you use most in a week?
(EP)
What was the last face-to-face conversation you had? Can you recall the facial expressions used? Did anything stand out? 
Have you ever had to control your facial expression (e.g., to be polite, to hide a lie, etc.)? What was that like? Was it hard? 
Can you recall a time you had trouble reading someone's facial expression? What do you think they were trying to convey?
(EX)
What do you trust more, someone's facial expression or what they are telling you? Why might someone think the opposite? 
Do you think it would be easier to communicate using only verbal cues or only non-verbal cues? Why? 
Eye contact is an important part of non-verbal communication – Do you think it is important to maintain eye contact throughout a conversation? Why might this be difficult for some people (e.g., perceived as rude)?
