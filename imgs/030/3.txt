Facts:
Koko, the gorilla who was taught sign language, once lied to her trainers. Koko blamed her kitten for tearing a sink out of the wall.
Alex the gray parrot is the first and only nonhuman to ask a question. While learning different colors, he asked "What color am I?" 
Researchers have recently decoded prairie dogs' calls and think they have the most complex nonhuman language. In a quick chirp they can describe a predator's species, size, and shape.

Do you think pets try and communicate with humans? In what ways might pets communicate with humans? What do you think these gestures (e.g., tail wagging, jumping, ears back, etc.) mean? 
In what ways do animals communicate with other animals?

(EP)
Have you ever trained an animal to understand certain cues? What was the process like? Was it successful? 
Can you recall a time that you ran into a wild or stray animal? What happened? 
Have you had a pet or seen a pet that misunderstood commands (sit when they're told to stand, rolled over when they were told to lay down, etc.)? What were they doing?
(EX)
Humans often apply human-like emotions (e.g., joy, sadness, etc.) to animals. Can you see any issues that could arise from this? What about benefits? 
Do you think animals/pets have different personalities? How can you tell? Why might someone think differently? 
Do you think our pets actually understand what we say or do they just learn to respond to our gestures or tone? Why might someone argue the opposite?
