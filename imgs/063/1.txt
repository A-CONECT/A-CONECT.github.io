People shopping at a local yard sale.
Facts:
On average, there are over 165,000 garage sales each week in the United States.  
Only a small percentage of older Americans downsize and do it for financial and/or health reasons.
A study has shown that the perfect age to downsize is 64 because people are generally mentally agile enough to deal with the buying and selling of property.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common reasons people downsize? 
What are some common items that people have two of when they move in together? 
When downsizing, what do you do with your extra belongings? 
(EP) 
Have you ever downsized before? Why were you going through this transition? 
Have you ever had a garage sale, or donated items to the Goodwill? When did you do this last? What did you get rid of? 
Did you ever move in with someone and realize you had too much stuff? How did you solve your space problem?
(EX) 
Do you think it is necessary to move into a smaller home after the kids move out? Why do you think that? 
Do you think people should have big houses and a lot of material things, or do you think it is better to live within your means and only have what you need? Why? 
What are some pros and cons of decluttering regularly? Do you think decluttering often is necessary? Why/why not?
