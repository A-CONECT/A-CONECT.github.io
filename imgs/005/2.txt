Topic: Voting RightsPicture Caption:
Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and his wife, Coretta Scott King, leading a black voting rights march from Selma, Alabama, to the state capital, 1965.
Facts:
Maine and Vermont are the only states that allow felons to vote from prison.
Ohio's constitution prohibits "idiots" from voting. 
Four years after the Voting Rights Act was passed, African-American voter turnout increased by almost 900 percent in Mississippi. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some important legislations relating to voting? 
What was the atmosphere like when the Voting Rights Act was signed? Did that impact your community? 
Can you name some leaders who pushed for voting rights?
(EP) 
Was there a certain event or cause that empowered you to vote? What was it like? 
Were there any marches or protests for voting rights that you witnessed? 
Were there any policies you voted for that did not pass? 
Were there any political nominees you voted for that did not win? Who were they? Why did you vote for them? 
(EX) 
Do you think voting is a good means for change? Should people vote? What are some good/bad things about voting? 
Why might someone choose not to vote? Are there any other effective means for change? 
Would you ever vote for something you didn't believe in? Why or why not? What are some reasons a person would vote against their beliefs? 
Do you think there have been political shifts since more individuals gained the right to vote? If yes, how so?
