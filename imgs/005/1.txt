Facts:  
In 1800, states chose their own voting days, meaning a Presidential election began as early as April and ended as late as October. 
Over 2 billion dollars was spent on the 2016 United States presidential race. 
Mickey Mouse is the most popular fake write-in candidate. To keep officials from counting so many fake votes, Georgia passed a law banning Mickey from the ballot in 1987.  
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Are you an active voter? 
Was voting important in your household growing up?  
How old were you when you first voted? What was voting like and how has it changed (e.g., punch cards, ballots, mail)? 
Have you seen or noticed the attitude towards voting change over your lifetime? 
(EP) 
Have you ever run for a position either in school or in your community? If so, how did you campaign? 
Do you remember a specific time when you had to be a leader? 
Are there any memorable elections that stand out to you?
(EX) 
Who was the first president you voted for? Why did you vote for this particular candidate? What qualities do you think make a good political leader? Why do you think other people voted for the other candidate(s)? 
Do you think politics is a good topic of conversation? Why might some people choose to (or choose not to) talk about politics? 
Do you think voting confidentiality is important? Why or why not?
