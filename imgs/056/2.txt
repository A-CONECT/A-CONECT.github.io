Facts:
For Jack-O'-Lanterns, people in Ireland and Scotland originally used beats or turnips.
The Haunted House Association estimates there are over 3,000 haunted houses in America. One-third of those haunted houses operate for charitable causes.
The largest temporary corn maze spanned over 60 acres of land. Several visitors in the maze called 911 after being lost in the maze for hours and worried the maze would close before they found their way out.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you have any Halloween traditions? 
What are some Halloween traditions you can think of? What is your favorite Halloween tradition? 
Do you enjoy scary movies? 
(EP) 
Have you ever been to a haunted house (or corn maze)? What was that like? If no, have you ever wanted to go to a haunted house? 
Did you go to any Halloween parties growing up? What games did you play? 
Have you ever carved a pumpkin? What did you carve? What was the process like?
(EX) 
In your opinion, is it important to practice holiday traditions? Why/why not? Do you think Halloween is an important holiday? Why/why not?
Why do you think people enjoy being scared? What makes you think this way? 
Traditionally, people pass out candy on Halloween. Do you think it is acceptable for people to pass out other things like toothbrushes or fruit instead? Why/why not?
