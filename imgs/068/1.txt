Facts:
Savings bonds are a popular gift for newborns because "one size fits all" and the gift "grows" in value as the child grows.
If your dog eats your savings bond, the bond can be replaced.
The most expensive stock was traded at about $336,000 in 2018.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Do you have any bonds? 
Have you ever given bonds as a gift? 
What are some different types of bonds? 
What stocks, if any, do you currently have? 
(EP)
Do you remember any stock market crashes over the years? Which ones stand out to you? What were you doing at the time? 
Have you ever seen your investments increase significantly in value? 
Can you think of a time when you discussed your investments with someone else? How did it go? 
(EX)
Do you think investing in the stock market is like gambling? Is there a strategy to investing? 
Do stock market crashes/booms affect your trust in the system? Do you think people should act on these events or wait it out? What might influence a person's decision? 
Would you be more interested in a job if stocks were part of your benefit package? Why/why not? Why do you think some companies encourage their employees to invest in their stocks?
