Facts:
Currently, the highest paid actor is Mark Wahlberg who took home $68 million in 2017.
The first Oscars were given in 1929 in a short 15-minute ceremony at a private hotel.
Charlie Chaplin typically improvised his stories in front of the camera, which led him to shoot hundreds of takes when making a movie.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Who are some of the actors and actresses you remember when growing up? 
What were some of your favorite movies growing up? 
Who are some of your favorite actors/actresses today?  
(EP)
What was your first movie experience? What was the movie? What do you remember about it? 
Have you ever had a movie experience where you were deeply affected (ex: made you cry, inspired you, etc.)? Was there a movie that shocked you? 
Did you, or anyone in your family, ever have a role in theater? What was the role and what was it for?
(EX)
Do you think actors these days are overrated? Are they paid too much for what they do? Why or why not? 
Compared to the past, do you feel that minorities are well-represented in the movie industry? Why/why not? How would you change this industry? 
Some actors and actresses are known for their excellent acting. Should casting in a movie influence a person's decision to see a movie? 
Do you think the acting alone makes a movie worthwhile? Why or why not? What other things make a movie what it is?
