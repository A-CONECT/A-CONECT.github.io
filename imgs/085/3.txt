Facts:
In 2017, 30% of the top grossing movies were sequels or prequels.
2016 set the record for highest percentage of movies with a female protagonist at 29%; this is up from 7% in 2015.
The first successful full-length movie in 1915 cost around $100,000 to make, while the cost to make movies today range from $100-$400 million.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What did a movie cost when you first started going to the theater? 
What were the popular theater snacks back then? 
Can you think of leading women in the movies over time? 
How has your experience of cinema change over time?
(EP)
Have your movie interests changed over time? How so? 
Have you seen a remake of a movie that disappointed you? Why did the movie fall short for you? 
What was the first movie that gripped you and made you want to see it repeatedly? What did you enjoy about that movie? How did it make you feel?
(EX)
Does censorship still exists in movies? Why/why not? 
Are movie rating systems still as relevant today as they were in the past? Why do you think that? 
Going to movies can be very expensive, and movie companies strategically plan out release dates. Has the goal of a movie, which is to entertain, changed over time? Or has money become the incentive? Why would someone think otherwise?
