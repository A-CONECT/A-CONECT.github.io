Facts:
In 1878, President Rutherford started the tradition of an Easter Egg Roll on the front lawn of the White House each year.
Half of the states in the United States have banned the practice of dying chicks colors for Easter.
In the mid-1800s, people in New York believed buying new clothes to wear on Easter would bring them good luck for the rest of the year.
