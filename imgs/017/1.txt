Topic: Chores as a KidPicture Caption:
A mom and her daughter folding laundry. 
Facts:
American kids average $780 in allowances each year. Less than 1% of those kids save any of their allowance money.
A recent study suggests that the majority of modern children prefer to do homework or chores than play outside.
Children who participate in household chores are more likely to achieve important milestones later in life, like starting a career. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Did you have any chores growing up? What were they? 
Were there any chores you avoided? 
Were you paid for your chores? Did you have other incentives for doing a good job?
(EP) 
Do you remember a time when you made a mess or broke something important? How did you handle this? 
How were chores assigned in your house? Did you ever have to compete with a sibling? 
Can you remember a time your family had to work together to complete a chore? What was that like? 
As an adult has your view of chores changed? 
(EX) 
Do you think it is important for children to have set chores in the household? Why or why not? What do you think chores teach children? 
Should children be paid in some way for doing chores? Why might someone think differently? 
Do you think it's important to keep a standard when it comes to chores? Is it about the effort they put into it or the actual outcome? 
How does the area you live in (ex: city vs. rural) influence what chores are assigned to you? Do you think gender also influences what chores you are assigned to you? Why or why not?
