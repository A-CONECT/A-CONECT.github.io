Graphic design students at the University of Bedfordshire in Luton, England, working on projects together.
Facts:
A diverse team can outperform a non-diverse team by up to 6 times.
The most productive team consists of 4-9 people. Larger groups tend to experience problems in areas like communication.
Research shows that the top two reasons for job satisfaction are effective leadership and personal interest. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What qualities make up a good leader? 
What qualities make up a good team member? 
Are there any qualities that are neutral, or could be good or bad depending on the situation? 
Do you know of any team building activities?  
(EP) 
Can you recall a time where you had to lead a team? What was that like? 
Have you ever taken part in a team building or leadership event for work or school? 
Are there any leaders you've admired? Do you remember a particularly admirable moment or event during their leadership? 
What events in your life helped develop your leadership skills?
(EX) 
In your experience, is working in a team more or less effective than doing a job yourself? What are the benefits you can think of for teamwork and for working alone? 
Do you think it's best for workers to be closely supervised or more loosely supervised? Why do you think that? 
Do you think that the work force in this country has moved towards a more cooperative work mindset or a more individual one? 
Do you think that creating a competitive atmosphere in the workplace helps to motivate people or discourage them?
