Facts:
The fastest recorded speed of someone skiing is about 157 miles per hour. 
The North Pole Marathon takes place on top of the frozen Arctic Ocean, making it the only marathon that’s ran on water.
Some competitions involve racing others down snowy slopes on unusual items, such as a wok pan or a shovel.  
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some activities people do in the snow? 
Do you have any favorite activities in the snow? 
Is there a snow activity that you’ve always wanted to try but never have? What about a snow activity that you tried and didn't like?
(EP) 
Can you recall a significant snowstorm that occurred when you were growing up? What was it like? What did you do? 
Have you ever experienced extremely cold weather? Where were you? 
Have you ever had a "snow incident" (ex: car stuck, sliding into something, etc.)? 
What is your favorite memory outside in the snow? 
(EX) 
In what ways can snow be a good thing? Why might it not be a good thing? 
Do you think people should be expected go to work/school if there is snow on the roads? In what circumstances should people stay home? 
Some individuals partake in high risk activities such as mountaineering and ice climbing. Do you think activities like these are worth the risk? Why might someone think otherwise?
