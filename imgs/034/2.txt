Facts:
The largest recorded dodgeball game took place at the University of California and had over 6,000 players.
Table tennis, or ping-pong, players can burn up to 500 calories per an hour of play.
Roller Derby is one of only four major sports created in America; with the others being basketball, American football, and baseball.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Was there a particular indoor activity that you enjoyed doing with friends growing up (ex: roller skating, basketball, etc.)? How has that changed from childhood to adulthood? 
Is there a set space in your home where you do activities? 
Do you listen to music or watch tv while doing some indoor activities?  
(EP) 
Have you ever been in a league (ex: pool, bowling, etc.)? Were there any memorable games? 
Have you ever hosted or attended a game night with friends or family? What was that like? 
Have you ever been to any local events that included fun indoor activities? What was it? What were the activities?
(EX) 
Do you think kids should only play high-energy games outdoors? Why/why not? 
Many children stay indoors to play video games versus going outside. Do you think that's bad for their health? Why might someone disagree? 
Parks offer a free way for people to be active outdoors. Do you think there should be cheaper/more accessible ways to be active indoors as well? Why might this be important?
