Facts:
Professional chess players can have up to 300,000 chess patterns stored in their memory.
Rubik's Cube designer, Ernõ Rubik, took a full month to solve the cube's puzzle.
There are 20 errors in the New York Times crosswords each year.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some games that require a lot of thinking/strategizing? 
What are some good strategies you use when playing thinking games? 
Do you play any puzzle games (ex: Sudoku, crossword, jigsaw, etc.)? What games?
(EP) 
Have you ever played a game where you couldn't keep track of all the rules? Why was that? 
Do you remember any educational games you did in school to learn concepts/facts? How did the game(s) work? 
Have you ever been particularly frustrated by a thinking game? What game? How long did you stick with it?
(EX) 
Do you prefer to play games that require a lot of thinking or games that are based off of chance? 
Is it more rewarding to win a game that requires strategy or a game based off of chance? Why is that? 
Why might people play thinking games? Are these games played purely for enjoyment?
