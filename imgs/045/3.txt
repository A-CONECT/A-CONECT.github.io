Oprah Winfrey helping cut the ribbon at the Leadership Academy, a school for girls in South Africa.
Facts:
Oprah credits her grandmother with guiding her towards success, saying it was Hattie Mae who encouraged her to speak publicly and be confident.
The United States is the number one country for female entrepreneurs.
On average, company founders are 40 years old when they start their companies.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some famous entrepreneurs that you can think of (Oprah Winfrey, Steve Jobs, Bill Gates, etc.)? 
What qualities make a successful entrepreneur? 
Can you think of any environments that encourage entrepreneurship (ex: farmers market, family companies, etc.)?
(EP) 
Have you ever sold anything that you've made? How did you find your customers? 
Do you know any local entrepreneurs? Do you have any favorites? What did/do they make or sell? 
Have any of your friends or family members attempted to be entrepreneurs? Were they successful? 
(EX) 
Do you think it has gotten easier or harder to be an entrepreneur in today's climate? Why might someone think differently? 
Do you think entrepreneurship requires new ideas? What about improving another's ideas? 
Do you think certain factors influence where a person shops more than others (ex: item quality, content, supporting local businesses, etc.)? Why might this differ from person to person? 
Does entrepreneurship require risk? How so? In what ways can this risk be avoided?
