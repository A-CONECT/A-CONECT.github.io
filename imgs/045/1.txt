People viewing “The Starry Night” painting by Vincent van Gogh at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City.
Facts:
In just 10 years Van Gogh painted over 900 paintings.
Leonardo da Vinci may have inspired the paint-by-numbers kits as he taught his apprentices to paint using number-sorted areas on canvases.
A study by the Arts Council found 41% of creative workers are self-employed.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some famous artists or art pieces you can think of? 
Are there any artists or art pieces that you particularly admire? 
Are there any artists or art pieces you don't care for? 
What art styles/types (ex: photography, painting, sculpting, etc.) do you enjoy most?
(EP) 
Have you gone to an art museum before? What do you remember about it? 
Have you ever made an art piece you were particularly proud of? What did you use to make it? 
Did you ever attempt an art project that didn't turn out the way you expected? What was that like?
(EX) 
How do we judge art? Do you think it's subjective? If so, why are some art pieces universally more influential than others? 
Should artists be limited by what is acceptable to society? Or should they push these boundaries? Why/why not? 
Should an artist paint for themselves or for their audience? Why might someone think differently?
