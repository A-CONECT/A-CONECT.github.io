Facts: 
Madeleine Albright, the first female Secretary of State, immigrated to the US from the Czech Republic.  
Research shows children who know their family's history have more self-confidence.
More than three-fourths of all immigrants speak English within 10 years of immigrating into the U.S.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some of your ancestors? 
Where did they live and what did they do? 
When did your family come to the United States? Do you know any of their stories? 
Do you have antiques passed down in your family?
(EP) 
Have you ever been to a family reunion? What was it like? Where was it? Who was there? 
Does your family have any important traditions? Can you tell me about how you learned any of these traditions or how you taught them to others?
Do you know of any relatives who were pioneers that moved westward during the 1800s? 
(EX) 
Do you think it is important for newly immigrated families to adopt their new country's traditions and culture or should they maintain their original traditions and culture? 
What are some of the hardships and/or opportunities immigrants encounter when entering a new country? 
What are the responsibilities of a government to take care of refugees and immigrants? 
What are some of the main reasons, in your opinion, that people choose to immigrate to other countries?
