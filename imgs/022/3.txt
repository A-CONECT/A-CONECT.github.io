Facts:
In the US, Churches are starting to use warm water for baptisms in an attempt to make it more comfortable for infants. 
In Thailand, Buddhist priests regularly bless new cars, motorbikes, and even new Thai Airways jets.
The Amish hold religious services in homes, rather than in churches. They believe that a church is defined by its people not its buildings.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some popular religious ceremonies? 
What are some religious ceremonies that you've attended over the years? 
If you haven't experienced a religious ceremony, would you want to? What would you expect?
(EP) 
Was there an important ceremony in your family? What did it entail? Is this something you carried on in your own family? 
Is there a religious ceremony that really stands out to you that you've attended? What was it?

(EX) 
Do you think there's some reason religious ceremonies occur at certain ages? How might the ceremony change if completed at an alternative age? 
Do you think some religious ceremonies should be exclusive? Why/why not? 
What purpose do you think religious ceremonies serve? Would you enjoy experiencing another religion's ceremony?
