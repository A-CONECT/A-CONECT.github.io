Students throwing their graduation caps up at their high school graduation.
Facts:
Graduates tossing their caps in the air began during a Naval Academy ceremony in 1912 because when students graduated from the academy, they were no longer required to wear their caps.
In Italy and Argentina, students are pelted with sticky food items by family and friends to celebrate their upcoming graduation.
Schools throughout the US, UK, and Australia participate in "Senior prank" days where the seniors organize pranks throughout the institution soon before graduation.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Did any of the schools you attended have award ceremonies? 
What types of awards did they give out to students? 
Did you attend graduation ceremonies growing up? 
Are there any common things you can think of that ceremonies have? 
(EP) 
Have you received an award at school? What was it for?  
If you've attended a graduation ceremony, what do you remember about it (ex: how large was the graduating class, outdoor vs. indoor)? Did you enjoy it? What was it like to watch or be a part of someone else's ceremony?
(EX) 
If you were able to choose what award you won at school, what would you want it to be? 
In your opinion is it important to recognize students by giving them awards when they go above and beyond? Why/why not? 
Do you think that by recognizing people for their accomplishments creates a healthy form of competition or makes others feel less about themselves?  Why do you think that?
