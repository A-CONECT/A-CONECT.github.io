Rosa Parks seated on the Cleveland Avenue bus in Montgomery, Alabama in 1955. 
Facts:
To decrease the amount of physical violence, a deal was created such that local law enforcement could arrest riders. This led the prisons to be filled with over 300 freedom riders.
Only 5 months after the rides started, the Interstate Commerce Commission changed their policies to allow anyone to sit where they wanted on a bus.
During the Freedom Rides, the goal was to arrive in New Orleans on May 17th; which was the 7th anniversary of Brown v. Board of Education.
