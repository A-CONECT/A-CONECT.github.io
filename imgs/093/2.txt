Facts:
In 1947, Jackie Robinson became the first African-American to play in Major League Baseball. He played for the Brooklyn Dodgers.
At UCLA, Jackie Robinson remains the only person to letter in four sports.
Jackie Robinson’s number 42 was retired throughout Major League Baseball. This was the first and only time a jersey number had been retired across an entire professional sports league.
