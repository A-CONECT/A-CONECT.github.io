Facts:
According to a neuro-developmental consultant, parents should sing to their children every day to avoid language problems developing in later life.
The word Karaoke means ‘Empty Orchestra’ in Japanese. It was founded when a band failed to show up for a performance and the owner asked his customers to come up and sing.
For the first 1000 years of Christianity, all hymns in the church were sung without instrumental accompaniment. This style of singing came to be known as Acappella; which means, “of the church” or “in the style of the church.”
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some of your favorite singers? 
Do you remember any songs or artists that your parents loved? 
Do you sing? What are some reasons why you sing? What songs do you like to sing?
(EP) 
Have you ever been part of choir in school? What about outside of school (ex: church)? 
Is there a particular song that, when it comes on the radio, you can't help but sing to it? Have you ever known someone who loved to sing, but didn't sing very well? 
Have you ever participated in karaoke? What was it like?
(EX) 
Do you think lip syncing is fraudulent or should it be okay because it is still the artist's voice? Why do you think someone would think otherwise? 
In the past, singers were recorded live; however today, some singers alter their voices with surgery or technology. Do you think singing today is becoming inauthentic? 
Similar to being an athlete, do you think some singers preform past their prime? Why or why not?
