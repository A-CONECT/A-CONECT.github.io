Topic: Changes in Parenting Over TimePicture Caption:
Father sharing his perspectives with his son.
Facts:
From 1965 to 2015, fathers have tripled the amount of time they spend caring for their kids. 
In the 1950s, experts would advise parents not to baby-proof their homes because babies would quickly learn what they shouldn’t play with. 
A study found that today’s children have more freedom to voice their opinions at home, but less freedom to play outside unsupervised.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
In what ways has parenting changed over time? Why do you think these changes occurred? 
How has parenting remained the same? 
What are some common expectations society has for parents and children?  
(EP) 
Do you remember any important life lessons your parents taught you as a kid? Was it around a specific event? How did they teach you? Did you teach the same life lessons to your kids, if you were a parent? 
What did you keep the same or change in your parenting style from your parents'? 
When was the last time you were a mentor or a role model for someone? What did you do?
(EX) 
Do you think children are able to get away with more today than when you were a child? Why/ why not? 
In the past, it was common for schools to be allowed to spank children. What are some pros and cons to this form of punishment in schools? 
Technology today impacts the interactions between parents and children. Do you think it makes their relationships stronger or weaker? Why might someone think differently?
