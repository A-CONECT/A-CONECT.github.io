Facts:
Tennessee has a juvenile court-operated school that keeps kids out of trouble. They require students to play basketball to teach them perseverance and cooperation.
30 countries have banned physical punishment of children in all settings, including the home.
A study found kids aged 4-8 are less likely to tell the truth when threatened with punishment. Kids were more likely to tell the truth if they thought it would please an adult.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common punishments for kids? 
Have you heard of any unusual punishments? 
What are some common punishments for adults? 
What types of behavior deserve a punishment (ex: not doing their chores, skipping school, arguing with a sibling, etc.)?
(EP) 
Have you ever witnessed an occasion where the punishment didn’t fit the crime? What happened? 
Have you ever seen someone "get away with something" that they shouldn't have done? What was it? 
Has it ever been your responsibility to reprimand someone or tell someone bad news? How did you approach the task? How did the person respond/react?
(EX) 
At what point does punishment become abuse? 
Should schools get involved if they believe a child is being abused at home? Why/ why not? 
Do you think punishments are effective in promoting change in children? Why/why not? 
What do you think about parents who choose to never punish or ground their child? What are the pros and cons of this?
