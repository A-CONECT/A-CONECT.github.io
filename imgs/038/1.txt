Mother giving her daughter a high-five as a reward for doing her homework.
Facts:
Research shows that positive feedback improves the performance of younger children more so than negative feedback. The opposite is true for older children and adults.
A school district in Fort Worth, Texas, gave a Chevrolet Camaro to the student who demonstrated the best attendance records and grades. 
Studies have shown that students who are encouraged to improve their grades alone tend to become less interested in learning as a result.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common rewards given to children for good behavior? 
What are some common rewards that adults get for good behavior? 
What types of behavior deserve a reward (ex: getting good grades, household chores, etc.)?  
(EP) 
What were some incentives to show good behavior when you were growing up? 
Can you think of a specific time you worked especially hard for a reward? Did you accomplish your goal? How?
Did your parents have an impact on your money saving habits, and/or what you spent money on? In what ways? Can you share an example? 
Have your views of rewards changed over time? 
Tell me about a time you were patient/impatient when working towards a reward.  
(EX) 
When should someone be rewarded? Should a person be rewarded if they try hard, or only if they succeed at the task at hand? 
Should rewards be used as a bribe/ incentive, or should they be used as a pleasant surprise? Why/ why not? 
Should children have allowances? What are some pros and cons to an allowance?
