Children playing “Duck, Duck, Goose.”
Facts:
Children whose schools offer outdoor classrooms or other forms of environmental education score higher on standardized tests.
The average child spends just under 5 hours a week playing outside, which is less than half the 11 hours their parents’ generation enjoyed. 
Tag and other chasing games have been banned in some schools due to concerns about injuries and how there is an unhealthy predatory element to the game.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Growing up, what games did you play outside? 
What types of playground equipment can you think of? 
Did you have a favorite playground in your community?
(EP) 
Did you play outside all year long or were there certain times of the year you weren't able to play outside? 
Can you remember a time when you got in trouble for playing a game you weren't supposed to play? What happened? 
Who did you play outside with the most? What was your favorite outdoor activity to do with him/her? Tell me about it. 
Were you a part of a club growing up? What was that experience like?
(EX) 
Do you think there is a certain age where a child no longer needs to be supervised while outside? Why that age? 
Do you think it's important to encourage children to play outside? Do you think children today spend enough time outside? 
How do you think game playing differs for children growing up in a city versus the countryside? Which setting would you prefer to have grown up in?
