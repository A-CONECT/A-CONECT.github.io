Facts:
The Barbie doll has held over 150 careers- including doctor, scientist, and lawyer- since her debut in 1959.
In 2014 LEGO Group became the world's largest toy company. Seven LEGO sets are sold every second.
Mr. Potato Head was the first toy advertised on television and originally, Mr. Potato Head had a real potato for a head that the customer had to purchase.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some classic toys that are still popular today? 
Are there any modern children's games or toys you have heard of? What are they? 
From your experience, how have toys changed over the years?
(EP) 
Did you ever hand-down a toy from your childhood? 
Can you think of a time when you had to be creative to entertain yourself with a toy/game? 
Was there a toy you particularly wanted as a kid? Were you able to get it? Why did the toy interest you?
(EX) 
Do you think the safety of childhood games/ toys has improved over time? In what ways? 
There are a lot more toy options today. Do you think this is a good thing or a bad thing? Why? 
Toys have become more specific in their purpose over the years (think Lego sets), in your opinion, has this change helped to expand children's imaginations or hinder them? What makes you think this way?
