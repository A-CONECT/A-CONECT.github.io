Facts:
According to Crayola, the average kid wears out 730 crayons by their 10th birthday.
A 2011 study suggests Hide-and-Seek encourages kids' development. By switching from hider to seeker they experience someone else's perspective.
Siblings who played video games together were found to have higher levels of sibling affection.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common games children can play indoors? 
What are some common toys children play with (ex: dolls, building sets, cars, etc.)? 
Are these games/toys different from ones you played with growing up? 
What were your favorite indoor activities growing up? 
(EP) 
Did you ever play hide-and-seek inside your house growing up? Did you have a really good hiding spot? 
Do you remember a time when you were very excited about winning a game? 
Would you rough house with your friends growing up? Is there a memorable time rough housing that you would like to share? 
Did you or your children have a favorite childhood toy? How did you get this toy? What made it so special?
(EX) 
Are there any board games that you don’t enjoy playing? Why don't you enjoy these games? Why do you think others enjoy playing these games? 
Do you think playing video games is as stimulating as playing board games? Why/why not? 
Are there any benefits of playing indoors instead of outdoors? Are there any disadvantages of playing indoors?
