Facts:
Pension plans became popular in the United States during World War II, when wage freezes prohibited outright increases in workers' pay. 
About 90% of public employees, and roughly 10% of private employees, in the U.S are covered by a defined-benefit plan today.
People who are age 70 or older can contribute up to $100,000 from their IRA directly to a charity.
