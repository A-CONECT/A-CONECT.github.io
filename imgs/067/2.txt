Facts:
More than 40,000 people claimed they had the same social security number in the 1940s. This was a result of a manufacturer using his secretary's social security number as an example card in the wallets he was selling.
The first monthly benefit check was in 1940 for a total of $22.54.
In a 2014 Pew Research survey, half of Generation X and Millennials said they thought they'd receive no Social Security benefits when they were ready to retire.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
How has social security changed over your lifetime? 
What are some ways the social security system could be improved? 
What other government assistance programs can you think of?
(EP) 
Have you ever had to use any state or government assistance programs before? What was that experience like? 
Do you know anyone that used social security before they retired? How did they use it? 
Can you recall a time where the instability of the government affected you or your family? 
(EX) 
Do you think that it's beneficial to have a government-run assistance program aimed at helping citizens? What are some of the pros and cons of this? 
Do you think it's a responsibility for people to pay into social security from their paychecks even if they never end up personally using it? Why do you think that? 
On average, people are living longer. In your opinion, has the social security system adapted to these changes in the population? If not, should it? Why might someone think differently?
