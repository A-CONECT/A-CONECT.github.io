Topic: Being a Role ModelPicture Caption:
Students and faculty showing their children where they work during the National Heart and Lung Institute’s “Bring your child to work day,” Imperial College, London.
Facts:
A recent study shows that kids rank their parents as the people they admire the most.
Teenagers have been found to have greater self-esteem and perform better in school when they have positive role models in their lives.
A 2013 study found having strong-willed friends can help an individual increase their self-control.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Who are some common role models for kids? 
What are some common reasons a child looks up to a role model? 
How can role models help to influence a child? 
(EP)
Can you remember a time when someone looked to you for guidance? Who were they? How did you approach the situation? 
Have you ever trained someone at your job? What was that like? 
Have you ever volunteered? When was the last time you volunteered? Where were you? What was it for?
(EX)
Some people have multiple mentors, while others just have one. Do you think having many mentors can be beneficial? Why/ why not? 
Is it important to have a guiding influence in a child's life? Why/ why not? 
Do you think that there is a responsibility for famous people or celebrities to set a healthy example for children? What makes you think this way? Why would someone think differently?
In your opinion, who's responsibility is it to set a good example for children?
