Facts:
Jorge Muñoz, an immigrant who became an American citizen, cooks and delivers food to dozens of laborers in New York free-of-charge. He finances the operation through his salary as a bus driver and donations.
Rosa Parks once said, "You must never be fearful about what you are doing when it's right".
In Greek Mythology a hero was someone who was half-god and half-human. The Greek word "heroes" meant "protector" or "defender".
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Do you have any personal heroes? Why is this person your hero? 
Who are some common personal heroes that people may have? 
What characteristics do you look for in a personal hero?  
(EP)
Have you ever had a "star struck" feeling when meeting someone you admire or look up to? Who was it? What happened? 
Did you have a personal hero growing up? At what moment did you realize they were your hero? 
How did these personal hero(s) influence your life? Tell me more.
(EX)
At the end of the day, personal heroes are humans too. Do you think that heroes are over glorified? Why/ why not? 
Some people may have fictional personal heroes (ex: Spiderman, Wonder Woman, etc.). What are some of the pros and cons to looking up to someone that isn't real? 
Do you think that it is important to have personal heroes as a guiding force in life? What makes you think/feel this way?
