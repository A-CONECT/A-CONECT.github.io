Facts:
A shopper who first purchases something on their list is more likely to buy unrelated items later as a reward.
Sears used to sell build-your-own homes in its mail order catalogs.
On average, it takes five trips to the mall to complete all holiday shopping.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What has changed in retail and grocery stores over time? 
Do you have any favorite brands or products? 
What are some ways for people to buy their groceries (ex: go to the store, delivery, order for pick-up, grow your own food, etc.)?
(EP) 
Is there an item you used to buy in the grocery store that isn't sold anymore? What was it? 
Was there ever a store that you always made an effort to stop by? Why? What did they sell? 
Did you or anyone you know work at a grocery store? What do you remember about this experience?
(EX) 
In your opinion, are items more cheaply made now than they were in the past? How might this benefit the consumer? What are some potential negatives? 
Do you think there are too many options at the store? Why might someone prefer many options? 
More grocery stores are promoting more convenient purchasing options. How do you think grocery stores will continue to change over time? Do you support these changes? What don't you like about them?
