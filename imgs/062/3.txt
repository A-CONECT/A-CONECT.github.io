Facts:
A recent study revealed that about 40% of people have re-gifted something.
In many Asian cultures, it is traditional to refuse gifts up to 3 times before accepting to avoid seeming greedy.
A yard sale in Las Vegas sold a sketch for $5 which later was found out to be an original painting worth $2,000,000.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Are there gifts you commonly give to people? 
What are some common homemade items to sell? 
What are some ways people can pass on household items that are no longer needed (ex: donate, re-gift, sell, throw away, etc.)?
(EP) 
Have you ever been surprised to receive a particular gift? What was the gift? What was the occasion? 
Have you ever had to pretend to enjoy a gift you weren't excited about? What was it? 
Have you ever sold something you made? What did you make? Do you know anyone who makes and sells items? Have you ever sold something that you ended up missing later on?
(EX) 
Do you think gifts represent how someone feels about a person? What are some pros and cons to having this perspective? 
In your opinion, do you think giving someone money in place of a gift is appropriate? Why might someone agree/disagree? 
Do you think it is important to always show gratitude when receiving a gift? In what instances might this be problematic?
