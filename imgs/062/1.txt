Facts:
One of the world's oldest handicraft is a sort of metal casting that has been used in India for over 4,000 years.
Teachers reported their most favorite gifts were handmade presents from students; however, homemade food was not recommended. 
David Babcock entered the Guinness World Record when he finished the Kansas City marathon in under 6 hours while knitting a scarf measuring over 12 feet long.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Is there anything you like to craft or make from scratch? 
What are some common crafts? 
Are there craft tools that you always keep on-hand? What are they?
(EP) 
What items have you made before that were a success? 
Have you ever made something that was a disaster? What happened? 
Have you ever made something out of strange or uncommon materials? What were the materials? What did you make? 
(EX) 
Would you rather receive a homemade item or a store-bought item? Why? Why might someone prefer the opposite? 
What are some pros and cons to making something from scratch as opposed to just buying it from the store? 
Do you think a license should be required to sell homemade items? Why/ why not?
