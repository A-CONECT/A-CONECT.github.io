Facts:
More than half of all adults say being an American makes up a lot of their personal identity.
In U.S., 55% of workers state they get a sense of identity from their job.
It is suggested that people do not achieve psychological wellbeing until age 82.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some things that shape personal identity (ex: media, music, culture, etc.)? 
How do you currently define yourself? 
In what ways has your personal identity changed over your life? 
(EP)
What experiences do you recall that had a strong influence on shaping your identity? 
Has someone ever thought something about you that surprised you? How did you react/respond? 
Have you ever consciously changed a behavior to appear a certain way? 
Do you have any items that you feel represent you?
(EX)
Do you think it is important to manage who or what influences your child? Are there limits to this? 
In your opinion, do you think it is important to have a variety of experiences to figure out who you are as a person? Why would someone feel differently?
Do you think it is important to surround yourself with like-minded people, or people with diverse views? What are the pros and cons?
