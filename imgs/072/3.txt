Topic: ConfidencePicture Caption:
An elementary school student speaking confidently in front of his class.
Facts:
In 2014, a survey reported that Americans boost their self-confidence with three methods: talking to a good friend, meditating and prayer, and working out.
In 2017, Less than 50% of men and women say they are confident in America's future.
Henry Ford once said, "Whether you think you can, or you think you can't—you're right". 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
How do you define confidence? 
What is the difference between confidence and arrogance? 
How can you tell when someone is or is not confident?
(EP)
What accomplishments over your lifetime have helped give you confidence? 
Was there an instance where you had to fake confidence in order to complete a task? What happened? 
Have you ever known someone who was particularly confident? How did their confidence make you feel? What was it like to be around them?
(EX) 
In your opinion, does confidence differ from person to person? Why might someone think differently?
Why might some people/groups feel more confident than others (ex: men vs. women, etc.)? 
Do you think growing up in different environments (ex: city vs rural) could play a role in how confident someone is? Why do you think this? 
Do you think it's important to support your peers and encourage confidence? In what ways might this be problematic (ex: creating an ego, over-confidence, etc.)?
