Facts:
Before it became known as the "Music City," Nashville, Tennessee was known as the Athens of the South. The city hosts a full-scale marble replica of the Greek Parthenon.
Washington D.C. is the only city without skyscrapers.
Tokyo railway operators have to literally push and cram rush hour commuters into subway cars due to the large amounts of people.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some big cities you've been to? Do you know any nicknames for certain big cities? 
Are there any cities that you haven't visited yet that you would like to? 
What makes a city a "big city"? Is there anything you feel your city is missing (ex: more parks, museums, etc.)?
(EP) 
Which large city that you've visited is your favorite? What is it about that city that you love? What things did you do while there? 
Have you ever had trouble navigating in a big city? What was that experience like? 
Have you ever witnessed a city change from a small town to a big city? How did this impact you?
(EX) 
What are some pros and cons of growing up/living in a big city? 
How much responsibility do you think a city has to controlling things such as air and noise pollution? 
What makes you think this? Do you think big cities should have regulations in place to help control over population or expansion? Why or why not?
