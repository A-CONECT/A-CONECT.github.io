Commuters waiting for a bus at Victoria station in London, England. 
Facts:
Public transportation saves 4.2 billion gallons of gas each year.
Areas with high public transit movement tend to have better overall security and reduced crime rates.
London's bus "Route 24" has remained unchanged since 1910, making it the oldest bus line in the world. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What transit systems are you familiar with? 
What kind of public transportation do you or have you used? 
What is your preferred or favorite mode of public transportation? Why?
(EP) 
What was public transit like in your city or town growing up? 
Do you have any interesting experiences while on public transportation? What happened? 
Have you ever taken a form of public transportation that was out of the ordinary? What was it? 
How have you seen public transportation change over the years?
(EX) 
Do you think that current security on transit systems is sufficient? 
What are the pros and cons of the public transportation in your city? 
In your opinion, should all cities have a well-developed public transit system in place? Why or why not? How can transit systems further benefit rural areas? 
Do you think that in America, people are more inclined to drive a car over taking public transit? What makes you think this? 
What do you think about biking and walking to work? What are the pros and cons of more people biking or walking?
