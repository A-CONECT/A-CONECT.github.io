Facts:
The first words ever spoken on the telephone were "Mr. Watson – come here – I want to see you" by Alexander Graham Bell to his assistant, Thomas Watson.  
There are still over 100,000 working payphones in the US. 
The first telephone book was just one page and listed 50 names. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some differences between phones in the past and today? What about phones has remained the same? 
What types of phones can you think of (ex: pay phones, car phones, mobile phones, land-line, etc.)? 
What are some other ways people stay in touch (ex: snail mail, email, video chats, etc.)? Which do you prefer?
(EP) 
Do you remember the first phone you had in your house? What was it? Who did you or your parents primarily call on the phone? 
What was your experience like learning new phone technology (ex: smartphones)? Was it easy to learn? 
Have you ever had or been a part of a prank phone call? What happened?
(EX) 
Today, people can use their cellphones for navigation, banking, and holding private information. What are some of the positives and negatives of having all your personal information on your cellphone? 
Phones continue to change over time. Do you consider these changes to be improvements? What are some possible downsides to new cell phone technology? 
Modern phones have an increasingly short life-span (ex: due to destructibility, software updates, etc.), how long do you think a phone should last? What's most important to you in a phone (ex: durability, different functions, etc.)? What phone features could someone else find important?
