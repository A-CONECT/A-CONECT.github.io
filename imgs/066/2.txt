Facts:
Only 10% of the world's currency is physical; the rest exists on computers.
The first portable computer was released in 1975 and weighed 55 pounds.
The first actual computer “bug” was a dead moth which was stuck in a Harvard Mark II computer in 1947.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you own a computer? 
What are some of the things that people use computers for? 
What different forms of computers can you think of?
(EX) 
When was the first time you saw a home computer? What were your initial thoughts? 
Do you remember the processes of learning how to use a computer? Was there anything you struggled with in particular? 
Have you bought a computer for a family member or friend? What was the process like of picking out the right one? 
(EP) 
Do you think the invention and progression of computers has made society better? Why do you think that? How has it made society worse? 
Do you think the brand of a computer matters? Why might someone think this?
In your opinion, do children spend too much time on computers these days? What are some of the pros and cons of children using computers at an early age? 
How has access to a computer changed over the last 30 years? What is currently limiting people from being able to access a computer?
