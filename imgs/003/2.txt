Topic: MLK ”I have a dream” SpeechPicture Caption:
Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. giving his famous ”I have a dream” speech at the National Mall in Washington, DC.
Facts:
Over a quarter million people attended Dr. MLK Jr.'s "I have a dream” speech. It was 17 minutes long. 
Dr. King skipped grades 9 and 12 to enroll in Morehouse College at the age of 15. 
George Washington is the only other American, besides Dr. King, who's birthday is a national holiday. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Is there anyone else you consider a civil rights leader in the news or in your community? Do you happen to remember their names? What did they fight for? 
How have civil rights movements affected your life and/or how has it changed the world?
What were some events of the Civil Rights movements? 
(EP) 
Do you remember the “I Have a Dream” speech? What do you remember about it?
How has racial discrimination affected your life? Tell me more. What happened? How did you feel? What did you do? 
Have you seen or attended any protests or demonstrations? Where? What happened? 
(EX) 
Dr. MLK was a strong advocate for non-violence. Do you think non-violent protests are effective? What are some of the benefits of non-violent protest? What are some limitations of non-violent protests? Why do you think some protests turn violent? 
What do you think it was like to be the head of a social protest movement? What are some challenges you might face being a social movement leader? 
In your opinion, is it important for young people to be active in social change in your community? Why or why not?
