Susan B. Antony (left) and Elizabeth Cady Stanton (right) working together as pioneers of the Women’s Suffrage Movement in the late 19th century.
Facts:
Susan B. Anthony was arrested for voting in the 1872 presidential election. She refused to pay her fine.
Today, women make on average of 79 cents to every dollar made by a man for the same work.
The top two IQ scores ever recorded belong to women.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some strong women that have been in your life? Why did you admire them? 
What work did the women in your family do? 
How has women gaining the right to vote changed American politics?
(EP) 
Do you remember the first time you voted? Who or what did you vote for? 
Did your family ever disagree on a political issue? What was the political issue? 
What women leaders in your life most stand out to you? 
Have you been in a leadership role yourself? What was that like for you? What happened?
(EX) 
There are certain professions that are typically associated with men or women. Why do you think there are some fields that are dominated by one gender? What are some reasons this should or should not change? 
Currently, many states do not give convicted criminals the right to vote. What are some reasons this might be a good idea? In what ways might this be unfair?
