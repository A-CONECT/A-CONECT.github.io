Topic: Cesar Chavez & the Labor Movement Picture Caption:
Cesar Chavez, leader of the National Farm Workers Association, working to improve labor rights. 
Facts:
Cesar Chavez used nonviolent strikes and facts to gain rights for migrant farm laborers.
In the '60s, Cesar Chavez led over a 300-mile march for farm laborers' rights.
Children can legally work on farms starting at the age of 12.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What was your first job? How much were you paid for your first job? 
Have you ever been part of a union? Do you remember the names of any unions? 
Were there any memorable strikes or boycotts in your lifetime? 
(EP) 
Have you ever participated in a strike or boycott or had one in your community? Can you tell me about that? 
Have you ever been treated unfairly by an employer? How so? 
Have you ever known a migrant farmer? What do you remember about them? 
Have you noticed any changes in farm-life or farming practices?
(EX) 
Some people say unions are bad for businesses. Do you think strikes or boycotts are a good way for workers to get their needs met? Why or why not? 
What are some benefits to going on strike or boycotting? What problems might a strike or a boycott cause? 
What are some pros and cons to modern farming at the industrial level?
