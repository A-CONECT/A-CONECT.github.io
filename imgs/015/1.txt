Topic: Learning to ReadPicture Caption:
A teacher reading a book with her students.
Facts:
A child needs to hear a word 250 times before they can remember it. 
Many children as old as eleven still want their parents to read aloud to them.
Over the past 50 years, the literacy of youth across the globe has increased by about 15%.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
How did you learn how to read? Did you use books, such as "Dick and Jane," to learn how to read? Can you remember any others? 
Do you enjoy reading? 
Do you have a favorite book? 
Is there a book that you would recommend to others? 
(EP)
Have you ever taught someone how to read? Do you remember a specific time where this was challenging? 
Can you recall a time where you had to read in front of an audience? 
Who primarily taught you how to read (e.g., teachers, parents, sibling, self-taught)? 
Did your parents, grandparents, or older siblings read to you as a child?
(EX)
Do you think it is important for parents to work with their kids on academics outside of school? Are there any reasons why someone might not want to do this? 
Children are often split up based on their reading level. Do you think this is a helpful teaching technique? Why or why not? 
Do you think learning to read other languages in the area (e.g., Spanish) is an important skill? 
How old do you think children should be when they start learning another language?
