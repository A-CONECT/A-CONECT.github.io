Topic: Learning CursivePicture Caption:
A young boy practicing cursive in his second-grade classroom. 
Facts:
The term 'cursive' comes from a Latin word that means 'to run.' 
Research has linked increased academic performance to good handwriting skills. 
Cursive is easier to learn for dyslexic students due to the fluid nature of this writing style. 
In France, learning to read and write in cursive is required. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Do you enjoy writing by hand? 
Do you think it is easier to write in print or cursive? 
Was there a letter in cursive that was particularly hard to learn or master? 
Do you find cursive more difficult to read than print?
(EP)
When was the last time you sent or received a handwritten letter? What was it? 
Do you remember practicing or developing your signature? Did you ever change it? 
Was there anyone's handwriting that you admired? Did you ever try and learn from them? Tell me about that. 
(EX)
Do you think good penmanship is still an important skill to have? Why or why not? 
Why do you think we learn cursive when we can just write in print? 
Do you think handwritten letters hold more sentimental value? Why or why not?
