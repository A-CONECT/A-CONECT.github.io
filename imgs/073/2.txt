Topic: Tutoring & MentoringPicture Caption:
Two students engaging in peer tutoring. 
Facts:
High school students raised their own reading scores as a result of tutoring fourth graders in reading. 
75% of private sector executives say mentoring has been critical in their own career development. 
In 2009, the South Korean government adopted measures to limit the number of hours students spent in tutorial centers in an effort to reduce childhood stress. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some people you would seek advice from? 
Where do you think mentoring takes place? 
For what reasons would someone want a tutor?
(EP) 
Have you ever tutored or been tutored? What was that like? 
Have you ever had difficulty learning a new concept? 
Did you continue to ask your tutor questions or pretend to understand?
Can you remember a time you mentored, or trained, someone at work? Did you find this experience to be rewarding? Tell me about that experience.
(EX) 
What are the pros and cons of being tutored? What are the pros and cons of teaching yourself? 
Do you think all schools have equal access to tutoring resources? Why/why not? 
Is it possible to fairly distribute tutoring resources among schools? Should both a mentor and a mentee get something out of the relationship?
