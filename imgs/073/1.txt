A man helping someone get their cart up the curb. 
Facts:
Netherlands has the highest percentage of people giving money to charities in the world.
April 28th is known as International Pay it Forward Day. People are encouraged to pay kindness forward through small acts; over 80 countries participate. 
About 55% of teenagers participate in volunteer activities which is nearly twice the adult volunteering rate.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who do you consider to be a "Good Samaritan"? 
What are some qualities that make a person a "Good Samaritan"? 
Why might someone be more charitable than another?
(EP) 
Do you remember a time where you helped a family member/friend, or a family/friend helped you? What happened? 
Have you ever witnessed someone doing a good deed? What did they do? 
Did your parents ever emphasize helping people or charitability? Can you remember how you learned or applied these lessons?
(EX) 
Do you think animals can be "Good Samaritans"? Is it possible for animals to have a moral compass? 
The Good Samaritan Act offers legal protection to people who try to aid others. Is this a positive act to have in place? What might be some potential obstacles/downfalls to this act? 
Should a person always take time to help someone, even if they are busy? Are there instances when someone shouldn't intervene due to safety concerns?
