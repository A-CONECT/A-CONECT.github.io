Facts:
One-in-three children adopted from foster care are adopted by parents of a different race.
Although less than 2% of Americans adopt, more than one-third have considered it.
The United States adopts more children than the rest of the world combined.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some common reasons to adopt? 
How might a couple show they are "qualified" parents and ready to adopt a child? 
How might the relationship between an adopted child and their parent be unique?
(EP)
Have you ever known someone who was adopted? 
Have you ever known someone who has spent time in foster care? Did they ever talk about their experience with you? 
How did you decide if you were going to have kids? Did you change your mind over time? 
Have any of your friends or family members considered adoption? 
(EX)
In your opinion, do you think it is important for parents who adopt a child with a different ethnic background to educate their adopted child on their heritage? Why might it be important for a child to meet or know their birth parents? Why might this be problematic?
Do you think adoption criteria are too strict? What are some pros and cons to strict regulations?
