Facts:
The proverb "It Takes a Village" comes from Nigeria and it exists in different forms in many African languages. The basic meaning is that child upbringing is a communal effort.
In 1997, Hillary Clinton received a Grammy Award for the audio recording of her book, "It Takes a Village".  The book focused on the impact individuals and groups outside the family have on a child's well-being.
About six-in-ten parents say that it’s hard to find child care in their community that is both affordable and high quality, and this is true across income groups.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
When you hear the phrase "it takes a village to raise a child," what comes to mind? 
How many people does it take to raise a child? Are some individuals more important than others? 
In what ways are children influenced by their community?
(EP)
Have you ever acted as a mentor for someone growing up? How did that happen? 
Was there anyone influential in your life that took you under their wing? Who was it/ how did they help you? 
Was there a house in your neighborhood you spent a lot of time at growing up?
(EX)
Is it important for a child to be actively engaged in their community? Why/why not? 
Do you think it is possible to have too many people raising a child? Why might this be problematic? 
How might families be stronger/ closer today than in the past? How might families be more disconnected and distant than in the past?
