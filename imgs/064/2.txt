Facts:
Whoopee cushions were invented by a 14-year-old Roman Emperor who used them frequently on guests.
The Frisbee originated in the 1950s when Yale students started playing catch with pie-tins.
A company called "Jagger and Lewis" promises that their ($200) smart collar will tell you your pup's mood.

(SM) 
Can you think of any unusual/fun kid toys? 
What are some unusual items that adults have? 
What is the weirdest invention you have heard of? What makes an item "fun" for you?
(EP) 
Have you ever received a gift that you didn't know how to use? What did you end up doing with it? 
Have you ever invented a game, or played a game that someone you know invented? How did it work? 
Have you ever watched infomercials about new inventions? Did you ever want to buy one?
(EX) 
Should useful inventions also be fun(or fun inventions also be useful)? How might an inventor take these priorities into account? 
Do you think weird inventions have an advantage in marketing because they are so unusual? Why/why not? 
Some inventions become rapidly popular and then disappear. Were there any short-lived trends throughout your life that you didn't enjoy? Why is that? Why do you think it was so popular?
