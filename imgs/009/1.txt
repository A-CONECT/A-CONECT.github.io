Facts:
India has a temple for rats called "Karni Devi." There are thousands of rats that are fed and worshipped.
Mice are used in almost 75% of all animal research procedures.
Some rodents use their tails for communication. Beavers slap the water surface with their tails to communicate and house mice rattle their tails to indicate alarm. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Have you or anyone you have known had a pet mouse or rat? 
Are you afraid of mice or rats? 
Have you ever lived in an area where rats or mice were abundant? 
Have you ever seen a mouse or rat in the wild? 
Are there any rats or mice from cartoons or stories that you remember?
(EP) 
Have you ever had a mouse or rat infestation? How did you control it? 
Are there any positive experiences you've had with mice or rats? What were they? 
Have you ever owned a pet that caught or/and ate a mouse or rat? What was that like?
(EX) 
Mice and rats are often used for science. Do you think there are benefits to this? What are some potential drawbacks? 
Even though rats and mice are seen as pests, is there an obligation to treat them humanely when they are used for science? Why or why not? 
Rats and mice have been carriers of deadly diseases in the past. Do you think this is something that we should worry about today? Why or why not?
