Children being introduced to a snake as part of ”Show and Tell.”
Facts:
Researchers are currently studying the properties of snake and spider venom in the hopes of developing therapies for things like strokes and cancer.
The hairs on a spider’s first pair of legs are sensitive to taste. As a result, spiders “taste” their prey by touching it. 
Snakes typically need to eat only 6–30 meals each year. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you have a fear of spiders or snakes? If you are afraid of either, is there a particular reason? 
Have you or someone you have known owned a pet spider or snake? What kind of spiders did you have around you growing up? 
Have you lived in an area that had a lot of snakes?
(EP) 
Have you ever had a frightening experience involving a spider? What happened? What about snakes? 
Have you had any interesting interactions with snakes either as a pet or in the wild? 
Are there any deadly species of spiders or snakes that you have encountered? What was that like?
(EX) 
Do you think the fear of spiders and snakes makes it harder for people to enjoy spending time outdoors? 
Do you think that spiders and snakes have gained a bad reputation through portrayals in movies and books? Why do you think that? 
What are some positive aspects to them (ex: roles in the environment)? Do you think knowing more about these positive aspects could help people feel more comfortable with them? Why or why not?
