Topic: Equal Pay (Wage Gap)Picture Caption:
U.S. Women’s National Soccer Team fans standing behind a large sign that reads, “Equal Play = Equal Pay,” to show their support for the players.
Facts:
As of 2016, women make on average of 80 cents for every dollar men make in the same field. 
Even though the wage gap between men and women has decreased over the years, it is predicted it will take another 70 years for it to completely close. 
"Bringing home the bacon" initially had nothing to do with income. 12th century churches would award bacon to a married man who swore before God that he had not argued with his wife for a year and a day. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Have you or anyone you've known been directly affected by the wage gap? 
Have you seen people taking jobs where they knew they were getting paid less than they should? Why do you think someone might do that? 
Do you think salaries have kept up with inflation over time?
(EP)
Can you think of a time when you thought you were underpaid for your work? Why do you think this was and how did it make you feel at the time? 
Did you ever take a job you did not want because you needed the work? Tell me more. 
Have you ever advocated for someone at work? What was that experience like?
(EX)
Do you think that there should be equal pay for men and women who do the same job at the same skill level? Why or why not?
In your experience has the wage gap lessened between men and women or grown? What makes you think that? 
Do you think there will be a time when men and women are both seen, and paid, as equals? What are some obstacles to this?
