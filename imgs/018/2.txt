Topic: Common Jobs for WomenPicture Caption:
A female emergency room nurse tending to a child patient.
Facts:
In the US, almost 90% of nursing positions are held by women. 
As of 2016, more doctoral degrees are earned by women than men in the U.S.
Since the 1950s, secretary and administrative assistant remains the most common job for women in the U.S. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
In your experience, what are some common jobs for women to have? What are some common jobs for men? 
What were the main jobs people had while you were growing up? Did these change as you got older? How so?
(EP)
What was your first job? 
What job or career did you have through most of your life? Were there women who also worked there? What roles or jobs did they perform? 
Growing up were women expected to hold certain jobs? What were these? 
Over your lifetime have you noticed a shift in the jobs that women perform?
(EX) 
Do you think men and women are expected to hold certain jobs? What are these? Where do you think these expectations came from? 
In your opinion, are there jobs that are better suited for men or women? Why do you think this is true? 
Should women (and men) have the opportunity to train and pursue any career regardless of expectation based on gender? Why or why not?
