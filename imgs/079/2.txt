Facts:
It was common for hospitals in Germany to have a brewery. Patients used to get a beer before going to bed.
Doernbecher Children's Hospital in Portland, Oregon, was established in 1926, and was the first full-service children's hospital in the Pacific Northwest.
The first artificial heart was successfully created and used in 1952 by Dr. Dodrill at Harper Hospital in Detroit, Michigan. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are local hospitals in your area (ex: Legacy, Providence, etc.)? 
What are common associations with hospitals (ex: sterile smell, full waiting rooms, etc.)? 
How have hospitals changed from when you were growing up (ex: technology, treatments)?
(EP)
Have you ever had any exciting events happen in the hospital (ex: child birth)? 
Have you or anyone in your family ever worked in a hospital? What was that like? 
Can you tell me about a time where your expectations for hospital care were/were not met?
(EX)
Do you think hospitals do enough to keep a sanitized environment? Could they do more? Why or why not? 
Does the high cost of medical care discourage people from getting proper medical services? Or is the high cost justified because of specialized and advanced treatments? What are some changes that could make care more accessible (ex: national healthcare)? 
Many people feel that medical care has become less personal. Is there less need for in-person appointments due to options like mail-order prescriptions and emailing doctors with medical questions? How do you think patient-doctor relationships will change in the future?
