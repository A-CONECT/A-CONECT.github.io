Facts:
Artificial eye lenses are the most common implanted medical devices with around 3 million procedures.
The first stethoscope was invented in 1816 and was basically an ear trumpet pressed on the chest.
There are roughly 1,700 different types of medical devices and 50,000 separate products. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What medical supplies do you think are necessary to keep at all times (ex: band aids)? 
What medical supplies have you used before (ex: blood pressure monitor, oxygen tank, etc.)? 
What are some improvements to medical supplies you think would be beneficial (i.e., making oxygen tanks less inconvenient, getting in and out of wheelchairs)?
(EP)
Have you ever helped someone use a medical device? What happened? 
Can you tell me how medical devices have changed over time? 
Are devices more efficient and useful for patients now than when your parents used them (ex: wheelchairs vs. automated scooters)?
(EX)
Do you think medical supplies are affordable? Why or why not? 
With the rise of medical technology, there has been an increase in companies manufacturing medical supplies. Do you think having so many choices is overwhelming or allows people a good variety for medical supplies? 
What are your thoughts regarding robots performing operations on patients instead of human surgeons? Do you think this would make healthcare more available?
