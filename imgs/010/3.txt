Facts: 
The cameras that captured the Moon’s surface for the first time were left there due to their weight. 
Every two minutes we take more pictures than the whole of humanity did in the 1800s.
The first photograph, taken in 1826, required 8 hours to produce a grainy image.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Have you ever owned a camera? 
What kind of cameras have you owned? 
Is anyone in your family/friend group a photographer? 
Do you enjoy taking photographs? What about it do you like?
(EP) 
Do you have any favorite childhood photos? What are they? Can you recall the experience/memory from the snapshot? 
Can you think of any embarrassing photos of yourself or a loved one? What about the photo or moment was embarrassing?
(EX) 
Does photography count as art even though you are using a device? Why would someone disagree? Could this device be considered a "tool" for art (ex: like a paint brush)? Why/why not? 
Some people think editing photos makes them less "real," do you agree with this? 
Most people take pictures with their cell phones, do you think that cell phones will replace regular cameras soon? Why/why not?
