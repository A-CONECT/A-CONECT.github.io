Topic: Changing JobsPicture Caption:
Three young adults awaiting a job interview. 
Facts:
80% of all available jobs are only found through contacting people directly. 
According to The Bureau of Labor Statistics, an average worker holds 10 jobs before the age of 40.
Walt Disney was fired as a newspaper editor because "he lacked imagination and had no good ideas.”
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What types of jobs have you had throughout your life? 
What changes have you noticed in the job sector? 
What are some reasons people change jobs? 
What did your parents do for work? 
What qualities do you look for in a boss/coworker? 
Do you have a favorite job? A least favorite job?
(EP) 
Do you have memorable experience from a job interview? 
Is there a work event such as a company party that you really enjoyed? Tell me about it. 
Have you ever had difficulties either training an individual, or being trained in the workplace? What was this like? How did you deal with it?
(EX) 
Adults today tend to change career paths many times. Why do you think this is? What are some pros and cons? 
Do you think work needs to be meaningful or purpose-driven, or is it more important to find a job that meets your financial needs? Why might someone think otherwise? 
Some jobs require teamwork whereas other jobs rely on employees to work individually. Which work style do you prefer? When might the other style be more suitable? 
How do you think technology has changed the job sector, for better or for worse? Why?
