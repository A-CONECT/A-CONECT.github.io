Topic: Promotion & Advancement Picture Caption:
Lucinda Cunningham, the first African-American female to be promoted to the rank of captain in the U.S. Coast Guard.
Facts:
According to a 2011 survey, about 60% of people who ask for a promotion, receive one. 
About half of workers believe playing "office politics" will help them get promoted.
The vast majority of executives say that having a sense of humor is important for career advancement.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What kinds of benefits did you receive from your past jobs? 
Why do people get promoted at work? 
What was your dream job growing up? 
How should bosses show appreciation to their employees?
(EP) 
Can you remember a time when you witnessed someone being treated unfairly in the workplace? What happened? 
Were you ever promoted at work? Were you expecting this promotion? How did it make you feel? How did you celebrate? 
What about a time when you thought you deserved a promotion, but didn’t get one? 
Do you have any favorite workplace moments? 
(EX) 
How do you think the experience of career advancement might differ for women and minority groups compared to white men? 
Do you think it is fair that some jobs have higher status and pay than others? 
Some people devote their lives to work. Do you think this is healthy? 
What do you think about work-life balance?
