Facts:
Seattle's Space Needle and Paris' Eiffel Tower were both built for World Fairs.
Alaska's State Fair has a cabbage weighing competition and the world record was set by a cabbage weighing over 125 pounds.
Cotton Candy was created in 1897 by a dentist. It was called "Fairy Floss".
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Are there any annual fairs in your community? What are they? 
What are some common fair activities or events (ex: parades, rodeos, games, rides)? 
What types of foods are usually served at fairs (ex: fried foods- corn dogs, elephant ears)? 
(EP) 
Do you remember the last fair you went to? What fair was it? What did you do? 
Have you ever won anything at a fair? What did you win? How did you win it? 
Have you ever participated in a parade? Which parade? Were you on a float or did you walk? 
(EX) 
Do you think fairs should provide healthy alternatives to the common fried or sugar-heavy choices? Why/why not? 
Do you think there are safety concerns when it comes to fair rides or are they "safe enough"? Why might someone think differently? 
Do you think it should solely be the parent's responsibility to look after their kids at the fair or should the fair be partly responsible? Why/why not?
