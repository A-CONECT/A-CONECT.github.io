Topic: Circus ActivitiesPicture Caption:
Four women performing a circus act as part of “Kurios,” a Cirque Du Soleil production.
Facts:
Sword swallowers practice their craft with spoons, knitting needles, and wire coat hangers.
14 to 21 clowns can fit in a compact car, known as a clown car. The car's interior is removed to give the car more space.
Cirque de Soleil has a custom-made trampoline that can launch a performer 35 feet into the air, which is twice as high as a regular trampoline!
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What activities come to mind when you think of the circus? 
Have you noticed or heard of any changes in the circus industry over your lifetime? 
Do you or anyone you know have any unusual/unique talents? What are they?
(EP) 
Have you ever been to a circus? If yes, what was your favorite part? Least favorite? 
Have you ever been to any other type of show (ex: comedy show, musical, etc.)? What did you see? What was it like? 
Do you remember the last time you saw a street performer? What were they doing? 
Have you ever been called on from the audience or volunteered to participate in an act or a show? What did you have to do? 
(EX) 
Do you think the circus exploits the unique talents and/or physical features of its participants? In what ways might this be true? Why might someone disagree? 
Do you think the circus has become less popular? Why do you think this is (ex: replaced by other forms of entertainment)? 
Do you think circus participants push the limits too far for entertainment (ex: fire eating, sword swallowing, contortion)? Why might someone think differently?
