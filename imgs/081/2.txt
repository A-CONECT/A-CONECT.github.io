Facts:
President Truman was the first person to enroll in Medicare. Truman’s wife, Bess, was the second person to enroll.
For the first Medicare enrollees, beneficiaries had an annual deductible of $40, and paid a monthly premium for only $3.
In the age 85 and over group in Medicare, there are more than twice as many women than men. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you think Medicare benefits are easy or hard to understand? 
What tool do you use to review and research your health plan (ex: phone, handbook and/or computer)? 
Can you think of any senior adult services that are provided in the US? What are they?
(EP) 
Have you ever had to switch doctor's because of a change in medical coverage? Have you ever had to switch prescriptions because of a change in coverage? What happened? 
Have your thoughts on health care coverage changed from when you were younger? How? 
Were you taught about how health insurance works when you were younger or did you have to learn when you got older?
(EX) 
Do you think government sponsored insurance programs such as Medicare and Medicaid are successful? Why/ why not? How could they improve? 
Do you think Medicare services should only be available for qualified individuals who are at least 65 years of age, or are there circumstances where Medicare should be available to individuals of a younger age? Why/ why not? 
Do you think the government should pay for some or all its citizens' healthcare? Why/Why not?
