Friends having lunch together.
Facts:
According to a study, highly social people are less likely to catch a cold and have a higher level of immune-system functioning.
About 50% of all people who come to a party in someone's house will check the medicine cabinet. But only 4% get caught.
A survey found more than one in ten Americans never entertain guests in their homes.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some common typical occasions people have company over? 
What types of dishes are common when people have guests over? Is there a dish you usually bring? 
Are there any common household rules associated with going over to someone's house (e.g. taking shoes off, not parking in the driveway, etc.)? 
What are some common activities that people partake in during home gatherings (board/ card games, listening to music, watching sports, etc.)?
(EP)
Have you ever hosted an event? What was it? 
Have you ever been to a work party? How was it? 
What other social events have you been to (holidays, movie/game nights, book club, etc.)? What were they like? 
Have you ever been to an awkward dinner party? Why was it awkward?
(EX)
Do you prefer being a host or a guest? What are some benefits to the other? 
Do you think there are certain standards that need to be met to be considered a good guest or host? What are they? Why might someone have different standards? 
In some social situations there is pressure to bring a "plus-one". Do you think this is always necessary? What are some circumstances where this shouldn't be expected?
