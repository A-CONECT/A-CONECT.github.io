Facts: 
Sleeping out on the porch was inspired by the idea that fresh air could help prevent, or remedy, diseases such as tuberculosis. 
In 2000, a 600-mile-long picnic was arranged in France to celebrate the first Bastille Day (a turning point in the revolution) of the new millennium.
The average person picnics at least three times a year.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common picnic foods? 
What would your ideal porch look like? What are some activities to do on a porch? 
What are some activities you do at a picnic?
(EP) 
Can you recall any family picnics or school picnics? Where was the picnic? 
Can you remember a time you were having a picnic outside and you had to move indoors? What happened (e.g., bees/ants were attracted to the food, it started raining, etc.)? 
Have you ever had a porch? Did you build it, or was it already there?
(EX) 
If you had to choose between having a backyard or having a porch, what would you choose? Why is that?
Picnics and porches commonly involve enjoying food, having company and relaxing. Is there anything about porches or picnics that you do not find enjoyable? How might picnics and porches bring people together?
