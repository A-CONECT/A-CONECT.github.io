Facts: 
Through license sales and taxes on equipment, hunters and fishermen pay for most fish and wildlife conservation programs.
In 2014, over 15% of the US population participated in fishing. 
Isle Royale National Park in Michigan has one of the largest moose herds remaining in the US.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common animals people hunt for? 
What are some different types of fish people eat? 
What are some different ways to prepare fish and meat?
(EP) 
Have you ever gone fishing? Do you remember catching your first fish? What was that like? What type of fish did you go fishing for? 
Have you ever gone on a hunting trip? Tell me more. 
Have you ever gone camping? Did you bring your own food or provide for yourself? What do you remember about the trip?
(EX) 
What are some of the reasons why someone would choose to hunt? Why might someone object to the idea of hunting? 
Fishing today requires purchasing a fishing license and following other state regulations. Do you think these restrictions serve their purpose, or are they an unnecessary expense? 
Is there something to be gained by knowing where you food comes from? Why/why not?
