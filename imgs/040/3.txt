Portrait of Louis Braille. 
Facts:
Louis Braille developed the 6-dot finger-tip reading system by the age of 15. 
Braille is not its own language, but rather a code that can be translated into many languages.
Almost 300 million people are visually impaired in the world, requiring visual support.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Have you ever seen Braille? Where? 
Do you know anyone with severe visual impairments? How does this impact their day-to-day life? 
What are some other types of aid provided to people with visual impairments?
(EP) 
Have you ever been very determined to overcome a learning obstacle? How did you tackle this obstacle? 
How inclusive was your school, or your children's school, of those with special needs? 
Did you know anyone that worked for or took part in a special education program?
(EX) 
Do you think schools should increase their accessibility to people with different disabilities (ex: visual, hearing, mobility)? How might they do this? What are some obstacles to this? 
How do you think people who have a vision or hearing impairment perceive the world differently? What might be some advantages/disadvantages to living with an impairment? 
What does the word "disability" mean to you? Given the achievements of Louis Braille, do you think "disabled" is a valid description? Why might someone think differently?
