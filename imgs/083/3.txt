A woman talks with a pharmacist about her medication. 
Facts:
On average, it takes 10-15 years to develop and bring a new prescription drug to the market.
Each year in the United States, more than 160 million prescriptions are written for antibiotics. 
About 70% of Americans are on at least one prescription drug. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some common mental health issues that require medication? 
What are some mental health medications you are familiar with? 
Have you or someone you know taken any of these? Have they helped? 
(EP)
Sometimes talking can be a form of "medication." Who do you turn to talk to about issues? What is it like opening up to them?
Have you ever had a bad side effect from medication? What happened? 
Have you ever been prescribed medicine but you felt like it didn’t actually help you?
When did medication help you a lot (ex: healing from an injury, etc.)?
(EX) 
Do you think that access to mental health medications should be free? How would these services be funded? 
What circumstances are medications necessary for mental health? Are there circumstances where medication is not necessary? 
Do you think medications are over-prescribed for mental illness? Why might someone think this? 
What other forms or treatment can be used to replace medication? Do you think doctors should explore these other forms of treatment before prescribing medications? Why or why not?
