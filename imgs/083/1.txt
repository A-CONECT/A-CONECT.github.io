Eddie Bocanegra (standing), the creator of the Urban Warriors program, speaks at a group therapy session that connects teenagers who live in high-violence neighborhoods in Chicago with war veterans.
Facts:
Use of therapy dogs date back to WWII to boost the mood and optimism of recovering soldiers.
Of the 97% of people who said mental health services are important, only 70% feel they have access to mental health care.
The United States spends $113 billion on mental health treatment, which is around 6% of the national healthcare spending.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some types of therapy (ex: couples therapy, individual therapy, drug therapy, etc.)? 
Why might someone attend therapy (ex: court-mandated, by choice, for their family, relationship, etc.)? 
What are some benefits to attending therapy? What are some risks involved in therapy?
(EP)
Has a friend ever relied on you for advice concerning a personal problem? What was the problem, and do you think you were helpful? 
Have one of your friends or family members ever seen a therapist? Did their therapist help them? Why/why not? 
Tell me about a time you saw therapy depicted in movies or television (ex: Dr. Phil, Good Will Hunting, and The Bob Newhart Show, etc.). When did you see it?
(EX) 
Some people attend therapy to help change bad habits. Do you think a person can change their habits alone, or is help needed sometimes? Why might some people think differently?
Do you think everyone could benefit from therapy or counseling? How might this benefit society? 
In the past there has been a stigma around seeking professional help. Do you think this is still true today? Why/why not?
