A young girl explores her sense of touch while blindfolded.
Facts:
The nose can smell at least one trillion distinct scents.
By age 20, half of one's taste receptors are gone. 
Early physical touch experiences are crucial for babies to develop a healthy body and cognitive function.  
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What senses do you value the most? Why? 
How has your palate changed over time? 
What fabrics do you find appealing to touch?
(EP) 
How did you learn about the senses in school? 
Has a scent ever triggered a memory? What was that like? What was the smell? 
Have you ever been to an aquarium that had a tide pool with animals to touch? What did the animals feel like? 
(EX) 
When you are picking food to eat, do you prioritize texture, smell, or the look of the dish? Why do some people not eat certain foods because of its texture? 
In the event of a sensory loss, to what extent do our remaining senses compensate? Why do you think our body and mind are able to adapt? 
In the future, how do you think technology will be used to improve our senses?
