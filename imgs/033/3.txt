A family enjoying a Chinese New Year's feast. 
Facts:
Traditionally, Chinese New Year's Eve dinner is very large and includes "lucky" foods such as fish, noodles, and dumplings.
Many Hispanic families celebrate Three King's Day with sweet bread for dessert. Hidden inside this bread is a baby Jesus figurine, which is an honor to receive in one's slice.
To celebrate the end of Ramadan, the Muslim holy month of fasting, there is a large festival where people give each other gifts and have a large meal together. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What holidays, if any, do you celebrate? 
Are the holidays you celebrate religious? 
Are there any foods you can't eat or always eat on holidays? 
(EP) 
Growing up, did you celebrate any holidays with people that held different traditions from your own? What were they? How did they make you feel? 
Did your family perform a ceremony or ritual before special meals? 
Is there a dish that you only make during special events? Why do you only make that for special events? Do people enjoy it?
(EX) 
Do you think it is important for families to pass on cultural traditions to the following generations? Why/why not? 
Some cultures engage in fasting during certain holidays. What do you think of this practice? Why? 
If someone didn’t respect your holiday traditions, would you still invite them to your holiday gathering? Why/why not?
