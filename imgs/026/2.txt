Facts:
The word "bully" was first used in 1530. At that time the word "bully" meant "sweetheart".
Approximately 160,000 teens skip school every day because of bullying.
Most bullying situations can be stopped by another student defending the bullied individual.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some examples of bullying? 
Where does most bullying take place? 
Are there different types of bullying?
(EP) 
Have you ever witnessed someone being bullied? What happened? What did you do? Would you do anything different? 
Sometime pranks can cross a line and be perceived as bullying. Have you ever seen this occur? Have you ever been a part of a prank? 
Have you ever witnessed bullying amongst adults? What happened? 
(EX) 
Do you think bullying is taken more seriously today than it used to be? Why/Why not? 
Bullying can include both physical and verbal harm. Do you think one is worse than the other? Why/why not? Should there be the same punishments for verbal and physical bullying? 
Why might someone bully? What are some reasons? 
Is it hard to intervene when you see bullying occurring? Why or why not?
