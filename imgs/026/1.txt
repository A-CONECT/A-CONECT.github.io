Topic: Playmates & FriendsPicture Caption:
Children playing in a treehouse.
Facts:
Kids behave better in the classroom when they have the chance to blow off steam on the playground during the day. 
Studies have shown that some animals can form friendships for life with individuals that aren’t from their species.
Over the course of seven years, we replace nearly 50% of our social network. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Did you have a lot of friends as a child? Who were they? What were your childhood friends like? 
Did you have favorite activities you shared with your friends? What did you do? 
Did you and your friends have common go-to activities or hang out spots? 
What qualities do you look for in a friend?
(EP) 
Do you have any memorable stories of times with your friends? What were you doing? 
When was the last time you saw your best friends? What did you all do together? 
Were there different social groups where you went to school? 
Was there a time you went out of your way to socialize with other people? What happened? What was your experience like?
(EX) 
Sometimes friends can drift apart, and sometimes they remain friends for life. What makes a friendship last the test of time? Why might friends drift apart? 
Cellphones and social media make staying in touch with friends much easier. What are some of the pros/cons of these new means of contact? 
Do friendships require effort, or can they develop naturally? Can you think of an instance where the opposite is true?
