Facts:
The Empire State building and Golden Gate Bridge were built by work relief programs established in response to the Great Depression. 
To keep spirits up, about 70 million people went to the movies each week during the Great Depression.
The first soup kitchen of the Great Depression was opened by Al Capone, the famous Chicago gangster.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What kinds of things happened during the Great Depression in the United States? 
Who were some of the main figures involved in the Great Depression and/or the New Deal? 
What things do you think brought the United States out of the Great Depression? 
(EP) 
Do you remember any habits that your parents had that came from their experience of the Great Depression? What were they? 
Do you remember any effects of the Great Depression on your family? What do you remember? 
What kinds of things happened to your community as it came out of the Depression (ex: jobs, building, prices, social-life, attitudes, etc.)? Can you give me an example you remember?
(EX) 
Do you think kids growing up in the Great Depression are mentally or emotionally stronger than kids growing up now? What advantages do children have now? What disadvantages do children have now? 
Social Security was created after the Great Depression. Do you think this was good for the country? What was good about it? What was not good about it? 
President Hoover was often blamed for the Great Depression. Do you think the President of the United States is responsible for what happens to the economy during their term? Why or why not?
