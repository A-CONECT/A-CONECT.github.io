Young nephew jousting with his uncle.
Facts:
The number of kids raised by extended family continues to increase. In 1993 nearly half of Illinois' children were living with extended family.
Unknown to most people, "nibling" is a collective term to refer to nieces and nephews.
There is a National Aunt and Uncle's day celebrated on July 26th each year.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Do you remember anything about your aunts or uncles? Do you happen to know their names? Where did they live? What were they like? What about nieces and/or nephews? 
What family traditions (or habits) does your extended family share?
(EP)
Did your aunt or uncle ever teach you anything? How did they teach you that? 
Did you ever visit your aunts or uncles? Can you tell me about that trip? 
Have you ever been to a family reunion? What was that like?  
(EX)
Do you think aunts and uncles should play a role in disciplining their nieces/nephews? Why/why not? 
What are some pros and cons of continuing family traditions (or habits) versus creating new ones? 
What are some good things/bad things about living near extended family?
