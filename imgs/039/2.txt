Topic: CousinsPicture Caption:
Third generation cousins playing together.
Facts:
Research shows that close friends are likely to share the same amount of genetic material as fourth cousins.
According to recent research, the average British person has nearly 200,000 living close relatives, most of which are cousins.
President Barack Obama and Vice President Dick Cheney are distant cousins.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
Were you close with your cousins while growing up? 
How many cousins did you have? What about second cousins? 
Was there a side of your family that you never got to meet? 
Do you celebrate special events with your immediate family and/or extended family?
(EP)
Was there anything that you enjoyed doing most with a cousin rather than a sibling or friend? 
Have you attended any events/celebrations with your cousins? What was it/what did it entail? 
Did you have any cousins that were considered outsiders in the family? 
(EX)
Some people choose to have smaller vs. larger families – can you think of any benefits or downsides to having a larger family? 
Do you think it's possible to choose your family (e.g., close friends) or do they have to be blood-related? 
Do you think it's important to have other people your age in your family to spend time with at family gatherings? Why/Why not?
