Facts:
In contrast to American culture, it is considered good manners in Japan to slurp noodles and soups because it shows your enjoyment.
Over time, certain actions that were considered manners no longer exist; such as, having the man order food for the woman at a restaurant.
62% of teens do not feel that chivalry is dead. 
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What are some common ways of being polite? 
What are some ways you practice being polite? 
What manners do you think are the most important to practice? 
(EP)
Have you ever known someone who was particularly impolite or had bad manners? What did they do? 
Who are the people that helped instill manners for you? How did they go about this? 
Is or was there a person who stands out to you as an example when it comes to being polite or having manners? What about them impressed you? 
How have you seen the practice of manners change over your life? 
(EX)
What is the difference between being polite and just being considerate or nice? Is there a difference? Why or why not? 
Is there such a thing as "too polite"? Why might someone think this? 
In your opinion, is chivalry (moral or social code) important? Why or why not? Why might someone think differently? 
Do you think that as society changes, what is considered polite changes as well (ex: cellphones or social engagement)? Why do you think this?
