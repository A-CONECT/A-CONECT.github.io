A preschool teacher helps two students resolve a conflict. 
Facts:
In 2008, US employees surveyed that they spent almost 3 hours a week dealing with conflict.
One study found couples were more likely to get into fights if one (or both) of the partners didn't get enough sleep.
Adult siblings can experience conflict and rivalry even if they no longer live with each other or their parents.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common topics you can think of that people disagree on? 
What are some common ways people resolve conflict? 
What ways have you used to resolve conflict in the past? 
(EP)
Was there any one you struggled to get along with growing up? Why was this? 
Have you ever held a grudge? What was it about? Did you ever manage to let it go? 
What is the most challenging argument that you have ever experienced? How did you work to resolve it?
(EX)
Is it better to avoid or engage in social conflict? What are some pros and cons to each strategy? 
In your opinion, is it sometimes better to withdraw from a conflict even if you feel you're "right?" What makes you think in this way? Why do some people act differently?
Do you think open communication is important (ex: expressing feelings, wants, concerns, etc.)? Why might open communication be challenging for some people? 
Do you think that lack of communication is the main cause of conflict? Why/why not?
