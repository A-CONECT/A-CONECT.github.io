Facts:
Extroverts are much more proactive in seeking out forgiveness than introverts.
The Amish are a very forgiving community and they practice forgiveness exercises with their families.
Studies have shown that when someone is apologizing to you, your blood pressure and heart rate decreases.  
Possible Questions:
(SM)
What does forgiveness mean to you? 
Has your interpretation of forgiveness changed over your lifetime? 
What are some common things people ask forgiveness for?
(EP)
Was there a person in your life who explained or taught you how to forgive? How did they go about this? 
Have you ever been asked for forgiveness? What was that like? 
Was there a time that you needed to ask someone to forgive you? Was it a difficult decision for you to make? 
(EX)
In your opinion, do you think some people deserve to be forgiven more than others? Why or why not? 
Do you think there are some acts that are unforgiveable? What makes you think this way? 
Why do you think it is easy for some people to "forgive and forget"? Is being able to forgive easily an admirable trait in your opinion? Why would someone think differently?
