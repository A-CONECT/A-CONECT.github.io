Facts:
Chocolate chips were invented after the chocolate chip cookie. Before then, chocolate shavings were used.  
The largest gingerbread house was built in Texas and it covered over 2,500 square feet. 
There is a 24-hour "cupcake ATM" in Beverly Hills, CA that dispenses custom cupcakes and cookies. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Do you know how to bake? Who taught you how to bake? 
Do you follow recipes or make it up as you go when you bake? 
Do you use a conventional or convection oven?
(EP) 
Can you recall a time you tried to bake something and it didn't turn out as you expected? What was different about it? 
Have you ever witnessed or been a part of a "baking disaster"? What happened? 
Who primarily bakes in your family household? What has been your favorite baked good that they've made? What was it for? 
Do you have any family traditions associated with baking?
(EX) 
"Baking from scratch" means something different to different people. What does this mean to you? Can you see how someone might interpret this differently (ex: ready-to-go pastry dough, canned filling, etc.)? 
Do you think baking is a science or art? Why would someone think opposite? 
Do you think baked goods should be healthy or indulgent? Why/why not?
