Facts:
Helen Keller wrote to eight Presidents of the United States, and received letters from all of them. 
Helen Keller was the first deaf and blind person to earn a college degree. She graduated from Radcliffe College, with honors, in 1904.
Traveling to 39 countries, from the UK to Syria, Helen Keller was a global advocate for educating blind, deaf, and disabled people.
