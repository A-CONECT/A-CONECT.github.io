Facts:
The North Carolina School of Science and Mathematics Food Drive collected over 500,000 pounds of food an 18-hour period. 
The author J.K. Rowling lost her billionaire status because she donated so much of her money to charity.
Seeing others give makes an individual more likely to give.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Have you ever donated an item? What was it? 
What are some common items that people typically donate? 
What are some annual food drives in your community?
(EP) 
Did you ever regret giving away an item? What did that feel like? 
Have you ever found any 'treasures' at Goodwill or Salvation Army? 
Growing up, did you rely on hand-me-down clothes rather than buying new? Why/why not?
(EX) 
Some charities prefer money to donated goods. Do you think this approach is too limiting? 
How do people ensure their donations are wisely spent? How does one decide who to give to? 
Do you think local farmers should donate a percentage of their fresh produce to food banks or food pantries in their area? Why/why not?
