A person on the street giving money to a homeless man. 
Facts:
In 2016, The United States spent around $49 billion in foreign aid.
The IRS found that people who earn less are more likely to give money to help others.
While more women are more likely to donate money to charity, men are more likely to volunteer or help a stranger.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Can you describe some qualities of a generous person? 
In what ways can a person be generous? 
Where do people learn how to be generous (ex: school, church, parents, etc.)?
(EP) 
Was there a time when you did something generous for someone else? Tell me about it. 
Has your generosity ever backfired? What happened? 
What's the most generous thing someone has done for you?
(EX) 
Do you think people with more money should give more to their community? Why or why not? 
America is known for giving aid to foreign countries. What do you think about this? 
Is generosity always a selfless act? What are some other motives?
