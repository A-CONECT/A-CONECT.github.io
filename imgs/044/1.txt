Facts:
Marie Curie is the only woman to have won more than one Nobel Prize for her Chemistry and Physics achievements.
Tu Youyou became the first Chinese woman to win a Nobel Prize in 2015 for her work in creating an anti-malaria drug that saved millions of lives in Asia and Africa.
Steven Hawking, a brilliant physicist, was a lazy student when he was younger and had the worst grades in his class at the age of 9.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some famous scientists you can think of? 
What are some major scientific breakthroughs you can think of? 
What area or areas of science most interest you?
(EP) 
Have you ever been to a science fair or science museum? What was that like? Did you have any favorite activities there? 
Have you ever built something science-related? What was it for? What did it do? 
Did you take science classes while in school? Do you have a favorite teacher/professor that made the subject matter fun? Tell me about that. 
(EX) 
Do you think necessity leads to a discovery, or do you think discovery comes from innovative thinkers pursuing a new directions? 
Should children be introduced to science in the classroom at a young age? What are some pros and cons to this? 
In your opinion, what is the goal of science? What makes you think this way?
