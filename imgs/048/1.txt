Facts:
It is estimated that this year Los Angeles Dodgers fans will consume over 3,000,000 hot dogs; setting the record for most hot dogs at one stadium.
One of the Oregon state fair’s most popular contests is the Milk Mustache contest.
The Kansas State Fair has started to recycle cooking grease used throughout the fairgrounds by taking to a nearby biodiesel plant and using it for fuel.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common foods served at a stadium or a fair? What are some common drinks? 
What types of events happen at stadiums (ex: sporting events, concerts, rodeos)? 
Do you think fair or stadium food has changed over time? Why/Why not?
(EP) 
Have you ever been to a concert or a professional sporting event? Where was it? Who was playing? Who did you go with? 
Have you ever received an autograph after a game or concert? Who was it? What did they autograph? 
Foods at fairs or stadiums are known for being unhealthy. Did you ever feel sick after eating too much of these foods?
(EX) 
Some venues have "beer gardens" in hopes to contain and regulate the consumption of alcohol. Do you think beer gardens do a good job of this? Why/ why not? Can you think of any other ways an event could contain and regulate alcohol consumption? 
Does food/drinks add to the socializing experience? Why/Why not? 
Should fairs and stadiums be allowed to independently control the prices of their food? Why/why not? 
Should people be allowed to bring their own food inside the venue?
