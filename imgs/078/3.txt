Facts:
Reading literary fiction can significantly improve one's capacity for empathy.
Almost 75% of students today rate themselves as less empathic than the average student 30 years ago.
By the age of 2, children display empathetic behaviors such as crying in reaction to other children in distress.
Possible Questions:
(SM)
How does one show empathy? 
How does one learn empathy? 
What are some situations where people show empathy?
(EP)
Have you ever been confused by another person's emotions or actions? Were you able to make sense of it later on? 
Has there ever been someone you always felt in sync with? What were some times that this happened? 
Have you ever felt alone while going through something? Would you have wanted someone to be empathetic towards you?
(EX) 
Do you think there can be consequences from being too empathic? Why/why not? 
Is it possible to fake empathy? Why/why not? 
Would it be better for someone to be honest about their lack of empathy? 
Do you think rights activists sacrifice empathy to further their cause? What about when activism turns violent?
