Facts:
Adults ages 45 to 64 are the most likely to be caregivers. 
In the U.S., family caregivers provide close to $400 billion in “free” care each year. 
Men currently account for 40% of family caregivers. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What attributes make up a good caregiver? 
How does caring for others make you feel? 
Who were/are your caregivers?
(EP) 
Have you ever had to take care of a family member? Was it easy for you to do? Why or why not? 
Were you an easy child to raise? 
Do you have any stories where you challenged your caregiver? 
Have you left your child or pet at a day care? How did you feel dropping them off?
(EX)
Why do you think people go into the caregiving profession (ex: teachers, home care, social workers, etc.)? What are some challenges? 
Why might someone provide care for others without payment? 
Do you think caregiving positions are underpaid? 
Kids often provide caregiving for their parents as they grow older. Should the children be expected to be the caregiver for their parents? What if they aren't able to (ex: due to finances or time)?
