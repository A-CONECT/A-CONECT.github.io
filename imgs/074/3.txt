Friends consoling one another.
Facts:
In 1982, Mother Teresa rescued 37 children trapped in a front-line hospital by creating a temporary cease-fire between Israel and Palestine.
A Ruby Tuesday employee noticed a woman eating alone on Mother's Day so the staff member not only treated her to lunch, but also surprised her with a gift basket. 
A study from Emory University found pleasure and reward areas of the brain were activated when participants helped others.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some qualities that make a compassionate person? 
What are ways people can help those in need? 
What are some organizations that show compassion (ex: Humane Society, Meals on Wheels, etc.)?
(EP) 
Do you remember a time when someone went out of their way to help you? How did you feel? 
Have you ever showed compassion towards a friend or family member? What happened? 
Do you think your level of compassion has changed with time? In what ways?
(EX) 
Do you think people wait until the holidays to show compassion? Does that make them a compassionate person? 
Is compassion a trait that can be taught or is it a quality you are born with? 
Do you think everyone deserves sympathy? What about people who make bad decisions?
