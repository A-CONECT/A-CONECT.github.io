Topic: SacrificePicture Caption:
A Mother feeding her child before she feeds herself. 
Facts:
Mahatma Gandhi undertook 17 fasts during India's freedom movement and his longest fast lasted 21 days.
Some people may be genetically hard-wired to help others, while some sociologists think a sense of selflessness can be instilled with practice.
As public servants, firemen take an important oath of "service before self."
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Why do people make sacrifices? 
What does making a sacrifice mean to you? 
Can you think of any historical figures who are known for making sacrifices?
(EP) 
Do you remember a time you put someone else's interests before your own? Did the outcome go the way you thought it would? 
Do you remember any sacrifices your parents made for the family? Why did they make that sacrifice? 
Was there a certain stage in your life where you felt particularly burdened by sacrifices? How did you handle that time?
(EX) 
Sacrifices seem to suggest a person has to give something up. Is it possible for someone to be happy from sacrifices? Do you think people ever regret making sacrifices? 
Can people successfully "have it all" without having to sacrifice anything? What does "having it all" mean? 
Is volunteering a sacrifice? Why/why not? Why might this be different for someone else?
