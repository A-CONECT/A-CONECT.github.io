Facts:
A survey found that people who are technology-obsessed are less focused, having an attention span of 8 seconds. Goldfish have an attention span of 9 seconds.  
96% of Americans will knowingly consume extremely hot food or drink that burns their mouth due to being impatient.
A bank survey found that despite customers rating themselves as patient, more than half reported that they hang up the phone after being on hold for one minute or less.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Where has your patience been tested (ex: DMV, extended family dinners, etc.)? 
What types of jobs require patience? 
What are some ways to cool off after you've lost your patience?
(EP) 
Have you ever been particularly eager for something to happen? How was the waiting process? 
Have you been surprised by someone's patience? Tell me about it. 
Can you remember a time when you were rushed, or running late, for something? What did you do? Were you worried about keeping someone waiting?
(EX) 
Bringing about larger change for communities or human rights can be time consuming. In these situations should people be patient? 
Do you think it's important to teach children patience? What do you think the biggest obstacle is to teaching younger generations this value? 
What are the pros and cons to being patient in a relationship?
