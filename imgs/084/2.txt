Facts:
According to "SHAPE" magazine, Portland has one of the top 10 best Farmers Markets in the United States.
An avocado has more than twice as much potassium as a banana.
Lemons are considered one of the world's healthiest foods as it contains a daily dose of vitamin C, cleanses the liver, boosts immunity and aids in weight loss.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are your favorite and least favorite vegetables? Fruits? 
What are some known super foods (blueberries, avocados, kale, eggs, etc.)? 
Where are some places people go to buy healthy foods?
(EP) 
Have you ever used a healthy recipe from a magazine? How did the dish turn out? 
Can you remember a time you tried an organic or vegan restaurant? 
Have you ever eaten a particular food to cheer yourself up? What was it? Did it help you feel better?
(EX) 
What is your health perspective: everything in moderation, strict adherence, or whatever you feel like? Why would some people do otherwise? 
How has the perception of "healthy eating" changed throughout the years? Are there any foods or diets that have remained consistent? 
Do you think a person can be healthy simply by their food choice? Why/why not? Can healthy eating replace exercise?
