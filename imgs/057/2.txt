Facts:
US scientists calculated that Santa would have to visit over 800 homes a second, traveling at 650 miles per hour to deliver all the world’s presents on Christmas Eve. 
In 2019, holiday shopping sales are projected to hit $1 trillion in the U.S.
During Christmas of 1914 (World War I), a truce was held between Germany and the UK. The soldiers exchanged gifts across no man’s land and played a game of soccer. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Growing up, how did your family open presents (ex: stockings first, one present before the holiday, etc.)? 
How much do you typically spend on presents? 
What are some common stocking stuffers?
(EP) 
Have you ever made a gift for someone from scratch? What was it? How did that go? 
Did you ever participate in "Secret Santa” or “White Elephant’”? What was it like? 
Did you ever receive the "perfect gift"? What was it? What is the worst gift you have ever received?
(EX) 
How much is 'too much' to spend on holiday presents? Why would someone choose to spend more or less? What are some pros and cons of holiday sales? 
Do you think it's important to give presents equally between children/siblings? Why or why not? 
Should you always be grateful when given a gift? Can you think of a circumstance where this wouldn't apply?
