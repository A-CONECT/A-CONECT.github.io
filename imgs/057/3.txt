Facts:
Kwanzaa is seven days long and on each day, a candle is lit and one of the seven principles are discussed.
In Iceland, there is a tradition called “Jolabokaflod”, which means “Christmas/Yule Book Flood”, where families exchange books on Christmas Eve. 
The Canadian Post has a "Santa Letter Writing Program" to encourage children to read and write letters, and do not require postage. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some common holiday family traditions? 
Do you have any favorite holiday music or songs? What are they? 
How can pets be included during the holiday season (ex: dressing them up, getting them in the family picture, buying them presents)? 
What do the holidays mean to you?
(EP) 
What holiday traditions does your family have? How have they changed over the years? 
Did/do religious traditions play a role in your holiday celebration? How so? 
Have you ever been to a holiday bazaar (market)? What did you buy? 
Growing up, did your family decorate your house with holiday decorations? What kinds of decorations did your family put up?
Have you ever gone to a holiday lights show? Where was it?
(EX) 
Do you think it's important for young kids to believe in Santa? Why would someone disagree? 
Many say that the holidays are a time for giving. Do you think it is important to volunteer during this time? Should it still be considered a genuine act if people only volunteer during the holiday season? Can you think of any pros or cons to volunteering during the holiday season (ex: taking time away from family if you barely see them)? 
At what age should parents stop buying their children gifts?
