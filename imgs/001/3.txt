Topic: National Symbols Picture Caption:
Tourists gathered around the Statue of Liberty in New York City. 
Facts:
Visitors needed to climb 20 stories of stairs in order to reach the crown of the Statue of Liberty. 
The Star-Spangled Banner melody comes from an old English drinking tune. 
At first, our founding fathers could not agree on a national bird. Benjamin Franklin strongly preferred a turkey. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What other national symbols can you think of? 
How have peoples’ attitudes changed towards patriotic symbols in your lifetime? 
What are some other things that define America that aren't national symbols, such as food, songs, and events?
(EP) 
Do you remember any patriotic celebrations you saw growing up? 
Have you ever visited a famous national symbol? If so, tell me more about your trip to visit the national symbol. 
Do you remember any patriotism in your family? 
Do remember a particular time in your life when you felt extremely proud to be an American?
(EX) 
Over the years, people have used American symbols, such as burning the flag or kneeling for the national anthem, to protest social and political issues. Why do some people think this is okay, while others do not? 
Should public displays of patriotism be mandatory? What are some reasons they should or should not be mandatory? 
What do national symbols mean to you?
