Topic: Early American Leaders Picture Caption:
The Committee of Five (John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Thomas Jefferson, Robert Livingston, and Roger Sherman) presenting the Declaration of Independence to Congress in 1776.
Facts:
A copy of the Declaration of Independence was sold for over $8 million in 2000. 
George Washington currently has over $300,000 in overdue library fines.
Victoria Woodhill was the first woman nominated to be a presidential candidate. She ran for office in 1872, before she had the right to vote. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Who are some of the early American leaders that people learn about in school? 
Which current American leaders do you like or dislike? 
What subjects in American history are interesting to you?
(EP) 
Have you ever been involved in any political or social change movements?
What is a political or social movement you felt strongly about and how did it make you feel?
Have you ever stood up to authority because of something you believed in? If so, what do you remember about the experience of standing up to authority?
(EX) 
Early American leaders established the United States as a democracy. What are some good things about living in democracy? What are some difficult things about living in a democracy? 
Should we speak up for our beliefs? Why or why not? 
Is there ever a situation where it would or would not be a good idea to stand up for what you believe in? When?
