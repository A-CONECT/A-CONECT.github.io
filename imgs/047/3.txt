Facts:
According to Google searches, the number one recipe people searched for in 2017 was Beef stroganoff. 
In India and Japan, an empty plate means you've enjoyed the meal, while in China, an empty plate indicates you weren't fed enough.
In South Korea nobody eats at the table until the most senior person takes a bite.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What are some of your favorite dinners? 
What are some quick ways someone can get dinner? 
What do you usually eat for dinner now? 
(EP) 
Growing up, did your parents enforce the "no elbows" on the table rule during dinner? What other rules did they enforce? 
What did dinner typically look like in your household over the years? 
When shopping for dinner, would you just get ingredients for that meal or for the whole week? 
Were there times where you were on a tight budget and had to stretch meals or ingredients? What were some of things you did to accomplish this? 
Did you ever have a dinner potluck with members of your community? How did it go?
(EX) 
Do you think eating at the dinner table together is an important habit for the family? Why might this be important? 
Do you think it is beneficial to eat dinner at a similar time every day? Why/why not? 
Do you think eating dessert is part of "dinner" or is it something extra?
