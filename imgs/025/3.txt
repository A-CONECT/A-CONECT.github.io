Facts:
Bricks are the oldest manufactured product.
Historically, clockmakers were the only craftsmen who were trained to design precise mechanical instruments. In fact, a German clockmaker invented the harmonica.
Some people think woodshop classes should be brought back into high schools because it would decrease the unemployment rate while filling valuable trade positions.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
What is the difference between a trade and a hobby? 
What are some trades you can think of? 
Did you or anyone in your family learn a trade? What trade? 
(EP) 
Is there anything you've built or helped build that you were/are proud of? What was it? What about the process did you enjoy? 
Is there anything you've attempted to build/make that didn't turn out the way you planned? What did you do with it?
Have you ever watched someone make/build anything that you admired? What was that experience like?
(EX) 
Some trades are commonly associated with a specific gender. Do you think there have been changes in the gender roles of trades over time? Why might someone be resistant to this change? 
Do you think it's important to learn a trade? What are some positives and negatives to learning a trade skill? 
Why do you think people go to trade school as opposed to college/university? Is one better than the other? Why might someone think differently?
