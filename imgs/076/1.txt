Facts:
Frank Warren created a community art project called "PostSecret" where individuals artistically design a blank postcard and anonymously mail their secrets to him. Over half a million secrets have been sent in so far. 
The average person is keeping 13 secrets right now.
John F. Kennedy was a superb golfer, but kept it a secret since he didn't want to seem unrelatable. He reacted in horror when he nearly hit a hole-in-one while running for President.
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
Why might someone keep a secret? 
What are some instances where keeping a secret is a good thing? 
What are some problems that might come from keeping a secret?
(EP) 
Did you ever have to keep a secret that you just really wanted to tell? What kept you from spilling the secret? 
Have you ever accidentally let out a secret? What happened? 
Has someone ever told one of your secrets? Did that change your relationship? How so?
(EX) 
When does keeping a secret become an example of lying? Could keeping information from someone be a form of lying (ex: Government, safety reasons, etc.)? 
Is there a difference between a child keeping a secret and an adult keeping a secret? In what ways are they the same? 
Do you think sharing secrets can create a bond between people or cause drama?
