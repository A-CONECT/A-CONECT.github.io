A group of coworkers exchange gossip in the office place.
Facts:
In a field experiment in 1955, researchers withdrew a student from a grade school class without explanation. Not surprisingly, rumors started to fly as students tried to make sense of the situation. 
Scientists have discovered that the brain releases significantly greater proportions of the "pleasure" hormone oxytocin, when we gossip. 
People felt a stronger bond with others they have gossiped with. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
How do rumors get started?
Why do you think people gossip? What are some common responses to gossip? 
Who would you trust with sharing gossip?
(EP) 
Have you ever been in an environment where people gossiped? How did you handle that situation? 
Have you ever accidentally spread a rumor? What happened? 
Have you ever heard gossip about you? How did that make you feel? 
Do you know anyone that gossips more than usual? Do you tend to watch what you say around them?
(EX) 
Do rumors/gossip in the media (ex: tabloid magazines, newspapers, social media) influence citizens' confidence in the government? Why do you think people are still drawn to this media material? 
How/why do rumors spread so quickly? As rumors are spread, do you think any truth in them become lost, like in the game "Telephone"? 
Why do you think people enjoy gossip? Are there any situations where gossiping can be positive or is gossiping always negative?
