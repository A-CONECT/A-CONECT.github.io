Topic: Truth & LyingPicture Caption:
President Nixon announces the release of edited Watergate transcripts.
Facts:
On average, people lie once or twice a day. 
60% of adults can’t have a ten minute conversation without lying at least once. 
Telling the truth when tempted to lie can significantly improve a person’s mental and physical health. 
Possible Questions:
(SM) 
How can you easily tell when someone's telling the truth or lying? Why might someone feel the need to lie? Are there instances where this might be helpful? 
Why might a government lie to its people? 
Are you familiar with other institutions that have lied?
(EP) 
Have you ever been caught lying? What happened? 
Has telling the truth ever gotten you in trouble? Tell me more. 
Have you ever known someone who lied a lot? What were they like? What did they lie about?
(EX) 
Do you think lying is okay when you are trying to be polite? What about a white lie? 
Do you think people tell the truth because of their personal morals, or to avoid a punishment? 
Should lawyers only represent people they believe are telling the truth? How would this affect people's right to representation in court?
